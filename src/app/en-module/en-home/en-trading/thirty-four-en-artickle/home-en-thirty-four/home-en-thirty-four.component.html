Stop-Limit Order

A stop-limit order is a combination of two types of orders: stop and limit. It specifies two prices: the limit price and the stop price, which may be the same or different. If the market price reaches the stop price, a limit order is placed.

Generally, stop-limit orders are used to control the amount of slippage on stop orders when entering a position on a breakout of a set level (remember, a regular stop order executes with arbitrary slippage).

A stop-limit order does not guarantee the execution of the trade even if the price touches the stop price (since the limit order might not trigger). For this reason, it is not recommended to use a stop-limit order to exit a position to limit losses (stop loss). Additionally, not all brokers accept this type of trading order.
