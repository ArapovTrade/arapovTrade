Main Reasons for Losing Money on the Stock Exchange

Trading Against the Trend

This is a common mistake among beginners. Lack of understanding of what creates trends in the market can lead to serious losses. It is important to have a theoretical foundation and a strategy to always be on the side of the winners.

Trading in One Time Frame

Not understanding market mechanics can lead to mistakes. It is important to always consider the "senior" time frame, as large players often act on longer periods. Observing different time frames helps to better understand the current trend.

Tilt (Emotional Influence)

Psychological aspects play a crucial role. Trying to quickly recoup losses can only worsen the situation due to unsystematic trading.

Premature Profit Taking

Inability to understand the current market phase and main trend can lead to premature position closures. Professionals try to stay in the trend until the end, as they are aware of the real market picture.

Inability to Adjust the Trading System

It is important to be able to adapt your trading strategy to changing market conditions. The lack of a clear trading system can make it difficult to understand market behavior.

Averaging Down

Averaging can be useful when used correctly in the direction of the main trend. However, without understanding the trend, a trader can accumulate too large a trading volume, leading to losses due to insufficient margin coverage.

Making "Huge" Trades

Attempting to make too large trades without sufficient margin coverage can lead to position liquidations due to margin calls. This is especially relevant during market corrections.

These main reasons for account losses in the market serve as lessons on the path to successful trading. Learning from others' experiences and developing your own strategy will help avoid common mistakes and start earning right away.
