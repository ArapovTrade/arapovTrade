Technical Market Analysis

Technical analysis is the most common type of analysis because most retail traders are not well-versed in economics and how it affects the value of an asset, so they rely entirely on price charts and the analysis of price quotes' behavior, specifically their direction and range of movement. In this section, we will learn about the main types of price chart displays, how to build "support and resistance" levels (horizontal/trend), as well as the main "price patterns" - patterns that for many traders are the "entry point into the market".

Technical analysis is the study of price charts to predict future price movements.

  • It involves studying market behavior based solely on price information and its graphical representations.
  • It is conducted to determine a trader's behavior in the market: whether to buy, sell a financial instrument, or stay out of the market, i.e., take no action.

Principles:

  • Price discounts everything.
  • The nature of price changes is not completely random.
  • Price changes in the market are systematic.
