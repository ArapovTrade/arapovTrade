Technical Market Analysis: Basics and Methods

Technical market analysis is one of the most popular methods for predicting price movements in financial markets. It is based on studying the behavior of market participants and analyzing historical data. Unlike fundamental analysis, technical analysis focuses exclusively on graphical information, making it accessible to a wide range of traders.

What is Technical Analysis?

Technical analysis is a method of forecasting future price movements based on the analysis of price patterns, levels, and indicators. Its goal is to determine the moments for opening or closing positions and to avoid losses in high-risk situations.

Key Principles of Technical Analysis

  • Price reflects everything: Any available market information is already factored into the price of an asset.
  • Price movements are not random: Price forms patterns that can be identified.
  • History repeats itself: The behavior of price patterns in the past often repeats in the future.

Objectives of Technical Analysis

Technical analysis addresses three main objectives:

  • Identifying trends: the direction of price movement (up, down, or sideways).
  • Finding entry points: moments when buying or selling an asset is most beneficial.
  • Assessing risk: determining levels where a trader should exit a position to minimize losses.

The Role of Volumes in Technical Analysis

Trading volumes are an essential tool for market analysis. They indicate how many trades were executed over a specific period and help traders understand the intensity of market participants' interest in an asset.

Why Are Volumes Important?

Volumes allow traders to:

  • Confirm trend strength: high volume during a price increase indicates market participants' confidence.
  • Identify trend weakness: declining volume during a price rise or fall may signal a potential reversal.
  • Determine entry and exit points: sharp volume spikes often precede significant price movements.

Volume Indicators

Popular volume indicators include:

  • Volume: Displays the total trading volume for the selected time interval.
  • On-Balance Volume (OBV): Considers the direction of price movement and volume to assess the overall trend strength.
  • Accumulation/Distribution (A/D): Analyzes the difference between closing prices and highs/lows to evaluate capital inflow or outflow.

Support and Resistance Levels

Support and resistance levels are key points on the chart where price often slows down or reverses. They are used to determine entry and exit points.

Horizontal Levels

Horizontal support and resistance levels are built on historical price lows and highs. These levels indicate where market participants are willing to buy or sell an asset.

Trend Lines

Trend lines reflect the overall direction of price movement. Upward lines indicate growth, downward lines show declines, and horizontal lines represent sideways trends.

Price Patterns

Price patterns are an essential element of technical analysis that help predict future price movements.

Triangles

Triangles form when the price range narrows. They can be ascending, descending, or symmetrical. A breakout from a triangle often signals the start of a new trend.

Head and Shoulders

The "head and shoulders" pattern indicates a trend reversal. It consists of three peaks, where the middle one (head) is higher than the other two (shoulders).

Flags and Pennants

These patterns indicate the continuation of the current trend after a temporary pause. They often appear after sharp price movements.

Advantages and Limitations of Technical Analysis

Technical analysis offers many tools for market operations but also has its limitations.

Advantages

  • Accessible and easy to use.
  • Suitable for short-term and medium-term trading.
  • Helps visually assess market behavior.

Limitations

  • Does not consider fundamental factors.
  • May provide false signals in low-liquidity conditions.
  • Requires experience and skills to interpret charts correctly.

Conclusion

Technical analysis is a powerful tool for market analysis and decision-making. Understanding trading volumes, support and resistance levels, and price patterns allows traders to effectively predict price movements and minimize risks. However, success requires combining technical analysis with other methods and continuously improving skills.
