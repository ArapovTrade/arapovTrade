Volume Market Analysis

This is the most in-demand type of analysis, as common sense tells us that most "large market participants" use "inside information" (insider info) in their trading to make decisions. And since they trade in large volumes — enough to determine their activity, retail traders can analyze their actions and join in. This type of analysis is given the most time in my training.

Volume market analysis involves analyzing the trading volumes at certain price levels and identifying patterns between the amount of money that has passed through a price and its subsequent movement.

Volumes are divided into two types: tick volumes and exchange volumes.

Tick volume is of no value to the trader as it is formed by the number of price fluctuations and has no relation to actual trades.

Exchange volume shows the number of futures and options contracts that have passed at a certain price. It is of two types: horizontal and vertical.

Horizontal volume

involves the use of cluster charts, where the number of trading operations is displayed as clusters opposite a specific price they relate to. This is how horizontal exchange volumes look in practice:

The chart shows red and green rectangles on the left side of the cluster — these are the familiar Japanese candlesticks. Inside them are horizontal rectangles with numbers that represent real trading volumes. If the figure is red, the number next to it indicates the number of sell trades at that price. If the rectangle is green, the number next to it indicates the number of buy trades.

Using volumes

Key aspects of volume analysis are moments of increased volume activity or decreased activity. Increased volume activity, large volume, volume spike — is such vertical volume that is easily identifiable on the chart without additional indicators and filters! On different timeframes, it looks about the same, and such volume always "stands out" — it is always "above average".

The relationship between vertical volume and price movement dynamics

The most important thing in understanding volume is the link "volume-price + result", often called "effort-result". We always link trading volume with price change to determine the direction of imbalance and the result of the "applied" volume. This link is used to assess the strength of a trend or a single wave, even individual bars!
