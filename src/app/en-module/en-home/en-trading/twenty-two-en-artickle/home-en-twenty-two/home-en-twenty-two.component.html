Economic Factors

The importance of economic factors is explained by the fact that the exchange rate of any currency directly depends on the economic development of the country and the measures by which this rate is regulated. Economic policy is determined by the interests of industrial and financial groups, exporters and importers of goods and capital, etc. In addition, given the increasing interdependence of countries in the world, optimal economic policy is a compromise between national and foreign interests.

The main economic factors that significantly influence the dynamics of exchange rates are economic indicators, negotiations, meetings of central banks, economic and trade unions, speeches by the heads of leading financial and economic state institutions, specialists in economics and financial and credit operations, agreements on joint interventions.

Economic indicators are important in three aspects:

  • expected level;
  • previous level;
  • range of forecasts.

In addition, it is necessary to consider the behavior of other markets: the stock market, the gold market, oil, and agricultural products. If the central bank replenishes reserves by buying securities for resale, the discount rate will decrease, which, in turn, allows banks to reduce interest rates and causes the exchange rate to fall. Conversely, reducing the country's reserves by selling securities for repurchase in the future leads to an increase in the discount rate, higher loan costs, and increased interest in savings in the national currency, which strengthens it.

In case of rising oil prices for oil-importing countries, an increase in demand for the US dollar can be expected, which will lead to its appreciation. A decrease in gold prices increases the purchase of securities, which leads to their appreciation and, accordingly, strengthens the national currency. Similarly, the cheapness of gold increases interest in deposits, allowing banks to control the growth of interest rates and increase demand for the national currency.

To more accurately assess the impact of a particular indicator on the dynamics of exchange rates, recent changes, expert forecasts, and the relationship between various indicators should be considered. It is expert forecasts that often provoke market reactions, which either intensify when the data is announced (if the forecast is confirmed) or, on the contrary, change to the opposite (if the forecasts are wrong).
