Position: Concepts, Types, and Categories

After a transaction is completed, a market position is formed, where changes in the exchange rate begin to bring profit or loss. A transaction results in the formation of an "open position," and the position is closed by performing a reverse transaction with the results being fixed.

When the base currency is bought, the open position is called a long position, and when it is sold, it is called a short position. A long position is opened in anticipation of the base currency's rate increase, while a short position is opened in anticipation of its decline.

Currency Positions

By duration, currency positions created for speculation are divided into two types:

  • Intraday Position

    The dealer opens and closes positions within the working day with settlements on one value date.

  • Strategic Position

    The dealer opens this position in anticipation of a significant rate change and holds it until closing over a certain period - from several days to several months.
