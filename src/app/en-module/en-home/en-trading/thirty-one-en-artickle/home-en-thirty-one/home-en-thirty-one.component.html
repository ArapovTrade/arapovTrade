Market Order

A market order executes immediately after reaching the exchange at the current price if there is an opposite limit order. For example, to execute a market buy order, a sell limit order is needed. To execute a market sell order, a buy limit order is needed.

To execute a market order, the best limit order available in the order book (closest to the Last price) is selected. Therefore, a market buy order is executed at the Ask price, and a market sell order is executed at the Bid price.

Market orders are used both to open a position and to close it (with profit or loss).

Market participants trading with market orders consume liquidity (other participants' limit orders). If we compare the exchange with a vegetable market, participants using market orders are like customers looking at price tags (limit orders in the order book) and agreeing (or not agreeing) with the prices indicated (with the only exception that on the market, customers usually only buy goods, while on the exchange, market orders can be used to both buy and sell).
