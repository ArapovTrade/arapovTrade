Types of Timeframes

Short-Term Timeframes

When choosing which timeframe is best for intraday trading, it is essential to first look at short-term timeframes. The most common short-term trading periods are:

  • M1 (One Minute)
  • M5 (Five Minutes)
  • M15 (Fifteen Minutes)
  • M30 (Thirty Minutes)

There is also a rarely used "tick" chart, which shows every price change in any direction. However, trading on such a chart, let alone making any forecasts based on it, is extremely difficult due to the high randomness of the data. There are strategies based on tick charts, but their successful implementation requires significant experience.

The most effective timeframes M1 and M5 are used in scalping, as the strategy involves analyzing the chart to obtain almost instantaneous profit from small price fluctuations, with trade durations of no more than a few minutes.

In the intraday strategy, the M1 period is rarely used due to its low predictability, which does not meet the requirements of trading with trade durations of up to several hours. The H1 timeframe is used in intraday trading primarily for reference purposes. The main trading periods for intraday traders are M5, M15, and M30.

Medium-Term Timeframes

In medium-term trading strategies, the most commonly used timeframes are:

  • H1 (One Hour)
  • H4 (Four Hours)

When predicting price dynamics for up to a few days, the most informative trading periods are considered to be H1 and H4. Charts with these timeframes allow for technical analysis and the identification of trading signals on this interval, without being too detailed, allowing for the ignoring of signals from small intraday fluctuations.

Long-Term Timeframes

Long-term traders, or investors, typically use the following trading periods for analysis:

  • Daily
  • Weekly
  • Monthly

There are also timeframes with intervals of several months and yearly, but they are mainly used for reference purposes, not for direct trading.

It is important not to get fixated on one or two timeframes, so as not to miss important aspects of the market's condition. Beginners are advised to start trading with longer-term timeframes and gradually move to shorter ones as they gain experience.

When choosing a primary timeframe, it is important to focus primarily on the chosen trading strategy. A too short timeframe will not be useful for long-term trading. It is crucial to clearly understand your trading strategy before opening trades to avoid losses.
