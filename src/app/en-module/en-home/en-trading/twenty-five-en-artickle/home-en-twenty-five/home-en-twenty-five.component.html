Key Economic Growth Indicators

GDP (Gross Domestic Product)

The gross domestic product (GDP) - the total volume of material goods created in the country in monetary terms. GDP growth leads to an increase in the currency of the country.

Industrial Production (Manufacturing Output)

The volume of industrial production for a certain period. Production growth leads to an increase in the currency.

Retail Sales

The volume of retail sales for a certain period. Stable growth in retail sales signals strong domestic demand and raises the currency.

Business Inventories & Sales

The volume of inventories and sales for production. Growth in inventories and a decline in sales - overstocking; can lead to a decline in production, which is a negative factor for the currency. Growth in inventories and sales - indicates an increase in business activity and turnover among entrepreneurs, which is a positive factor for the currency.

Durable Goods

The volume of ordered and transported durable goods (in billions of dollars). This indicator fluctuates significantly depending on the situation in the aerospace and defense industries.

Wholesale Sales & Inventories

The volume of wholesale trade sales and inventories.

New Home Sales

The volume of new home sales in the USA.

Existing Home Sales

The volume of home sales in the secondary housing market. Like the previous indicator, it is a serious indicator pointing to the level of welfare of citizens, the degree of use of mortgage lending, and the level of satisfaction with the current interest rate.

Factory Orders

The volume of raw materials and components ordered in the production process. An increase in orders indicates production potential.

Budget

Approval and execution of the budget by the governments of leading countries. Planning, approval, and execution of the budget with a surplus indicates the efficiency of the government, the ability to increase spending, including government investments, which underpin GDP growth.

Construction Spending

Construction spending in the private sector.

Construction Expenditures

Construction spending in the corporate sector.

Leading Indicators

The leading indicators index contains 12 components. The index allows predicting business cycles of economic development.
