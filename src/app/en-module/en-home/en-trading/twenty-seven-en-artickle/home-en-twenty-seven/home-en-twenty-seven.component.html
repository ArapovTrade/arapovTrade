Technical Market Analysis. Main Types of Chart

Bar Chart

Each bar has four indicators:

  • The "Opening Price" is always on the left.
  • The "Closing Price" is on the right.
  • The bar tail shows the "Lowest Price" for the bar's time period.
  • The top shows the "Highest Price".

Candlestick Chart

The difference between the "Candlestick Chart" and the "Bar Chart" is that the candle body shows the closing direction of the price. It is colored depending on the display settings, from the "opening price" to the "closing price".

Line Chart

The name already conveys the essence, the price change chart is expressed as a line, where each new point is the "closing price" for a certain period.

Main Types of Trends in the Market

"Bullish" Trend (Uptrend)

If you connect two consecutive low points on the chart (points that correspond to support levels), and you get a line with a positive slope (directed from the bottom left to the top right corner of the chart), this line is called the support line of the uptrend. If the subsequent low points are also on this line, the support line becomes more significant for the market. The more low points are near the support line, the more likely the subsequent low points will also be near this line. In the example of the USD/JPY quote, the thick line is the support line.

"Bearish" Trend (Downtrend)

The situation with the downtrend is similar. If you connect two consecutive high points on the chart (points that correspond to resistance levels), and you get a line with a negative slope (directed from the top left to the bottom right corner of the chart), this line is called the resistance line of the downtrend. The resistance line essentially defines the downtrend. If you draw a line parallel to the resistance line through the lowest point on the chart, both lines define the down channel.

The example of the USD/JPY quote chart shows the resistance line as a thick line. This line, together with the parallel dashed line passing through the lowest point on the chart, forms a down channel. You can also see that the price approached the resistance line several times and bounced off it, confirming the established downtrend.

Trading in the down channel is similar to trading in the uptrend — you need to buy currency at the lower boundary of the channel and sell currency at the upper boundary. It is important to understand that any downtrend will eventually reverse, so it is important not to miss the trend reversal signal.

"Sideways" Trend

which is sometimes called a "flat", is characterized by no noticeable upward or downward trend. In this case, the price fluctuates, but it is not possible to determine the support line of an uptrend or the resistance line of a downtrend. On the chart, this situation appears as a horizontal range (sideways channel) and is represented by two parallel horizontal lines between which the price fluctuates. The horizontal range is shown in the example of the USD/JPY quote.

Trading within the horizontal range is undesirable as a clearly defined trend has not yet formed, and it is easy to open a position in the wrong direction. The horizontal range can be understood as an equal confrontation between "bulls" and "bears". The longer this confrontation lasts, the stronger the subsequent price breakout will be in one direction or another, forming an uptrend or downtrend. The horizontal range is often observed on hourly charts of weekends and holidays when trading in the market is almost non-existent.

Trend periods can be short-term, medium-term, and long-term.

Level

is a horizontal line drawn through the area of values where the price changed its direction.

  • Support Level (SUP) — a line drawn through the minimum price values from below.
  • Resistance Level (Res) — a line drawn through the maximum price values from above.

Support and resistance levels are very important in the technical analysis of the FOREX market. The more often the price returns to a certain level, the more significant this level is for the market. This is explained by the psychology of traders who attach more importance to a level if it is confirmed by the price returning to it over time.

It often happens that if the support level is broken, it becomes the new resistance level. Or if the resistance level is broken, it becomes the new support level. The example of the USD/JPY quote chart shows the support and resistance levels.

The first level from the bottom is the support level and has been confirmed by the market twice. The same can be said about the second level from the bottom.

Note that the third level from the bottom is initially a resistance level and later becomes a support level. The fourth level from the bottom is a resistance level and has also been confirmed by the market twice.

It is important to understand that support and resistance levels are not exact values, and their determination is a creative task. Each trader determines the criteria for defining levels in their own way.

A level is considered broken if the closing price of the trading period breaks this level. However, historical data on FOREX currency quotes shows many examples where the closing price broke the level but returned to it in subsequent trading periods. Therefore, breaking levels alone is not a buy or sell signal — other technical analysis tools must also signal for such a decision.

To trade successfully on FOREX, technical analysis tools must be used in accordance with the current situation.
