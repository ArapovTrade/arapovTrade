Separation of trading systems. Automation

Manual Trading Systems

The trader independently analyzes the market and makes decisions on trades.

Pros:
  • The trader has complete control over the trading process.
  • Humans better account for minor market changes.
Cons:
  • The trader's psychology affects trading more than the trading system's rules. "Fear," "greed," "laziness," and "fatigue" become the trader's main enemies.

Mechanical Trading Systems

Trading is entirely handled by a robot, which follows all the rules of the trading system.

Pros:
  • The robot is not subject to psychological issues.
  • It never gets tired.
  • The robot precisely and accurately follows all the rules embedded in it.
Cons:
  • The robot cannot adequately respond to market changes, so even minor changes can lead to losses.
  • The trader must have programming skills to fully control the robot's work and make necessary adjustments promptly.
  • Basic technical problems (power outages, internet disruptions, etc.) can disrupt the robot's operation and lead to losses.

Semi-Automatic Trading Systems

The advisor only suggests trades to the trader, and the trader makes the final decisions.

Pros:
  • The trader controls every trade.
  • The main, routine work is handled by the advisor.
Cons:
  • Programming skills are also important.
  • The trader may begin to trust the advisor too much, dulling their reaction to minor market changes.
