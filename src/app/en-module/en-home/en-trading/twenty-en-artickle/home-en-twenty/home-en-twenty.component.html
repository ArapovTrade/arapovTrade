Analysis of Supply and Demand Within the Concept of Peter Steidlmayer

Introduction to Peter Steidlmayer’s Concept

The analysis of supply and demand is one of the key aspects of successful trading in financial markets. Understanding how market participants react to different price levels allows traders to find profitable trading opportunities. One of the most effective methods for such analysis is Market Profile, developed by Peter Steidlmayer, a trader at the Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT).

Unlike traditional technical analysis methods, which focus on indicators and chart patterns, the concept of Market Profile is based on studying price behavior through volume and time. This allows for a more precise determination of supply and demand zones, the fair value of an asset, and potential levels for trend reversals or continuations.

Key Principles of Market Profile:

  • Fair Value: Identifying price levels where trading volume is the highest, indicating an equilibrium zone between buyers and sellers.
  • Supply and Demand Imbalance: Detecting moments when the market moves out of equilibrium, creating conditions for an impulsive price movement.
  • Dynamic Price Behavior: Analyzing how the price reacts to key volume and liquidity levels.
  • Liquidity Zones: Identifying areas where large players conduct major operations, influencing the asset's price.

In this article, we will examine in detail how to apply Market Profile for effective market analysis, what principles it is based on, and how to use it to forecast market movements.

What is Market Profile?

Market Profile is a powerful market analysis tool that helps traders understand volume distribution at different price levels during a trading session. It represents a vertical histogram that highlights the zones where the price has remained the longest and where key volumes are concentrated.

Unlike traditional candlestick or line charts, Market Profile reveals market structure, allowing traders to analyze price movements through the lens of supply and demand. This is particularly useful for identifying support and resistance levels, as well as detecting high liquidity zones.

Key Components of Market Profile:

  • POC (Point of Control): The price level where the highest trading volume occurred over a given period. It often acts as a strong support or resistance level.
  • VA (Value Area): The fair value range, encompassing 70% of the total trading volume for a session. Traders use it to determine optimal entry and exit zones.
  • Initial Balance (IB): The price range established during the first hours of the trading session, setting the market’s movement tone.
  • High Volume Nodes (HVN): Price levels with high volume concentration, which can serve as consolidation points.
  • Low Volume Nodes (LVN): Areas with low trading volume, which often act as levels where the price moves quickly.

Using Market Profile enables traders to analyze market behavior more accurately, predict trend changes, and identify reversal points with high probability of success.

How Does Market Profile Reflect Supply and Demand?

The primary function of Market Profile is to identify zones where the price is in equilibrium between buyers and sellers, as well as to determine areas of imbalance, which can lead to strong trending movements.

Unlike standard technical analysis indicators, Market Profile visualizes volume distribution across different price levels, helping traders analyze supply and demand interaction in real time.

Principles of Supply and Demand Balance:

  • High Liquidity Zones: At price levels with the highest trading volumes, the price tends to consolidate, forming key support and resistance levels. This indicates that buyers and sellers agree on the current price.
  • Low Liquidity Zones: When the price moves quickly through certain levels, it signals weak supply or demand. These zones often act as potential breakout levels.
  • Value Area: These areas show where most transactions took place, making them crucial for determining key entry and exit points.
  • Trading Ranges and Breakouts: Market Profile helps identify when the price remains within a range (a consolidation phase) and when it is preparing for a breakout into a trending movement.
  • Imbalances in Market Profile: When the price moves beyond the fair value area, it signals an imbalance between supply and demand, potentially leading to a strong market move.

Analyzing supply and demand with Market Profile allows traders to make more informed entry and exit decisions, assess trend strength, and find optimal points for placing stop-loss and take-profit orders.

Application of Peter Steidlmayer’s Concept in Trading

Market Profile is a powerful analytical tool widely used by professional traders to identify areas of market participant interest, determine the balance of supply and demand, and predict possible price movements. Unlike traditional technical analysis, which relies on historical price data, Market Profile focuses on volume and the distribution of time spent by the price at specific levels.

Main Ways to Use Market Profile in Trading:

  • Identifying Supply and Demand Levels: Analyzing areas with the highest trading volumes (Point of Control, POC) helps traders find key levels where reversals or trend continuation are likely.
  • Finding Fair Value Zones (Value Area): Traders use the Value Area (the zone where 70% of all trades occurred) to understand where market participants consider the price to be fair.
  • Determining Resistance and Support Levels: Zones with a high concentration of volumes can act as barriers to price movement. If the price frequently tests the POC level, it may indicate its significance.
  • Identifying Breakout Points: When the price moves beyond the fair value zone, it signals a change in market balance and the potential start of a trending movement.
  • Determining Market Imbalance: If the price moves quickly through a low-volume area (Low Volume Node), this may indicate weak market participant interest in these levels, making them potential breakout zones.
  • Analyzing Market Dynamics: By comparing the current market profile with previous sessions, traders can identify recurring patterns that signal a potential trend change.

Market Profile is particularly effective in futures, forex, and stock markets as it helps better understand the structure of supply and demand.

How to Analyze Supply and Demand Imbalance?

The imbalance between supply and demand is one of the key factors that determine price movement in the market. When demand exceeds supply, the price rises, and when supply dominates, it declines. Using the concept of Market Profile, traders can identify areas where large orders accumulate and predict potential changes in market structure.

Key Indicators of Supply and Demand Imbalance:

  • Value Area Expansion: If the fair value zone (Value Area) expands, it signals high liquidity and the possibility of forming a strong trend.
  • Point of Control Shift (POC Shift): When the Point of Control (POC) moves up or down, it indicates a shift in market participant preferences and may signal trend continuation or reversal.
  • Volume Accumulation Outside the Value Area: If large volumes accumulate outside the fair value zone, it may indicate preparation for a breakout and a sharp price change.
  • Low Volume Nodes (LVN): Low-volume areas (LVN) often become levels where the price moves quickly, as interest in these levels is minimal.
  • Behavior at Value Area Boundaries: If the price repeatedly tests the boundaries of the value area (Value Area High and Value Area Low) but does not move beyond them, it may indicate that the market is in a state of equilibrium.

Practical Application of Imbalance Analysis:

  • If POC and the Value Area expand upwards, this confirms an uptrend and the possibility of opening long positions.
  • Narrowing of the value area and a downward POC shift may indicate a decline in liquidity and a potential market reversal.
  • Detecting abnormally high volumes near significant resistance or support levels may signal an impending breakout or rebound.

By analyzing supply and demand imbalance through Market Profile, traders can make more informed trading decisions, minimize risks, and increase the probability of successful trades.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Market Profile Analysis

Advantages:

  • Deep Market Understanding: Market Profile allows traders to analyze the actions of large participants, identify accumulation and distribution zones of liquidity.
  • Clear Support and Resistance Levels: Unlike traditional technical analysis, Market Profile shows actual volume concentration zones, helping traders make more informed decisions.
  • Flexibility: The tool can be applied to intraday trading as well as medium- and long-term strategies.
  • Detection of Market Anomalies: Market Profile helps identify moments when the market moves out of equilibrium, which may indicate the start of a trending movement.
  • Adaptability to Different Instruments: Market Profile analysis can be used in futures, forex, and cryptocurrency markets, making it a universal tool.

Disadvantages:

  • Complexity of Mastery: Effective use of Market Profile requires a deep understanding of market structure, making it less accessible for beginners.
  • Lack of Automated Signals: Unlike indicators like RSI or MACD, Market Profile requires subjective analysis, increasing the likelihood of errors in interpretation.
  • Inefficiency in Low-Liquidity Markets: Under low liquidity conditions, Market Profile levels may lose their significance, reducing analysis accuracy.
  • Additional Tools Required: Full use of Market Profile often requires a combination with other analytical methods, such as volume profiles, order flow, or the order book.
  • Platform Dependency: Market Profile is not available on all trading platforms, and its proper usage requires specialized software.

Despite certain drawbacks, Market Profile remains a powerful tool for analyzing supply and demand, allowing traders to make more informed trading decisions and effectively manage risks.

Market Profile Analysis Examples in Practice

Let’s examine how to use the concept of Peter Steidlmayer in real market conditions. Market Profile analysis helps identify key supply and demand levels, determine entry and exit points, and predict price behavior based on market structure.

1. Reversal Scenario

When the price stays in the fair value zone for a long time and then moves beyond it, this signals a shift in the balance of supply and demand. This scenario is often accompanied by an increase in volume and may lead to a trend reversal.

How to trade:

  • If the price breaks below the value area with increasing volume, it may indicate the start of a downtrend.
  • If it breaks upwards with confirming volume, a bullish trend may emerge.
  • A retest of the breakout zone can provide a low-risk entry opportunity.

2. Trend Continuation

In cases where the price breaks through the value area and continues to move towards an imbalance, this indicates a strong trend. Under such conditions, retracements to key Market Profile levels can be used to find entry points.

How to trade:

  • Monitor the POC (Point of Control) — if it shifts in the trend direction, it confirms the move.
  • Identify high liquidity zones (HVN) for taking profits.
  • Use low liquidity zones (LVN) to find potential breakout points.

3. Identifying Strong Levels

Market Profile analysis helps find important levels where large volume is concentrated, as well as zones with minimal liquidity. These levels can serve as reference points for placing stop-loss and take-profit orders.

How to trade:

  • Use low-volume zones (LVN) as potential resistance and support levels.
  • Track POC movement to determine whether supply or demand is increasing.
  • Open positions as the price approaches significant levels and observe the reaction at these points.

Understanding the concept of Market Profile and applying it in practice allows traders to adapt to changes in market structure and find the most profitable trading opportunities.

How to Use Market Profile for Successful Trading?

To effectively apply Peter Steidlmayer’s concept in real trading, it is important not only to understand the theoretical aspects of Market Profile but also to correctly interpret its signals depending on the market situation. Here are the main recommendations for traders:

  • Analyze Volume: Identify areas with maximum buyer and seller activity. High-volume areas usually indicate consolidation, while low-volume zones suggest a possible breakout.
  • Monitor POC and VA: Point of Control (POC) and Value Area (VA) help identify the most significant support and resistance levels. If the price approaches these levels, there is a high probability of a reversal or breakout.
  • Determine Trending and Ranging Markets: In a trending market, POC and VA levels shift in the direction of price movement, while in consolidation, they remain stable. This helps determine whether to enter a trend or wait for a breakout.
  • Use Market Profile in Combination with Other Tools: To increase signal accuracy, combine Market Profile with volume analysis, candlestick patterns, and Fibonacci levels.
  • Test Strategies Before Live Trading: Use a demo account or trading simulators to understand how Market Profile works in different market conditions before applying it to a real account.

Mastering Market Profile requires practice, but this tool can significantly improve the accuracy of trading decisions and enhance the understanding of market mechanisms.

Unlike traditional technical analysis, Market Profile provides a deeper understanding of market structure, revealing areas of liquidity accumulation and distribution. By using POC, VA, and imbalance zones, traders can effectively determine entry and exit points, as well as assess the likelihood of price breakouts or reversals.

Despite its complexity, Market Profile remains one of the most accurate methods of market analysis. Combining it with volume analysis, order flow, and candlestick patterns can significantly improve trading efficiency.

If you want to study Market Profile in depth, it is recommended to test it on a demo account, analyze historical data, and gradually integrate it into your trading strategy. Only regular practice and market pattern analysis will allow you to successfully apply this concept in real trading.
