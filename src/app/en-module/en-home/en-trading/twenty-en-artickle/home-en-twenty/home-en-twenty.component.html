Fundamental Market Analysis

Fundamental analysis involves studying various factors that affect the state of the economy as a whole, specific industries, as well as the position of a particular enterprise in the market with the aim of predicting market movements. When trading stocks, fundamental analysis is conducted at several levels, starting from the macro level to the level of a specific enterprise:

  • analysis of the current state and development prospects of the company;
  • analysis of the current state and development prospects of economic sectors;
  • study of the state of the national economy as a whole;
  • assessment of the impact of external factors, both economic and political;
  • analysis of natural factors.

Advantages and disadvantages of the market analysis method based on the principles of fundamental analysis:

The undeniable advantages include the fact that it helps to predict long-term development trends and assess the current state of the economy and finance.

The disadvantages (difficulties of use) include the fact that each company must be analyzed individually, which entails a fairly large volume of analytical work. Moreover, it is not so easy to separate factual information from information of an advertising nature or information distorted to some extent by the source of analytical reviews.

For a trader, it is important to know the future changes in exchange rates, that is, to be able to analyze the market and predict its direction and scale of development. Market analysis involves studying the factors that affect its development and the formation of a new exchange rate.

Fundamental analysis studies the changes in exchange rates under the influence of fundamental, economic, and political factors to determine the general trend of market development. The goal of fundamental analysis is to determine a fair rate that corresponds to the overall state of the market. It is predominantly used when working with long-term strategies based on trends lasting several months or even years. It is the analysis of fundamental indicators and their relationships that allows determining the trend almost at the very beginning of its life cycle. Fundamental analysis alone is not enough to determine the optimal moment for a transaction.
