Trading System

An essential topic for any future trader, as I mentioned earlier, having a trading system creates a stable income from the market. Without it, trading would be like playing a "casino game" where the winner is, of course, the casino. Below are the main types of trading systems, the categories they fall into, and how they differ from each other. Their pros and cons. In my courses, I teach a "trend trading system".

A trading system is a set of decision-making rules (trading or analytical) based on the signals of the market analysis tools used by the trader.

A trading system should answer the following questions:

  • What is happening in the market right now?
  • What might happen in the time frame that interests the trader?
  • What should the trader do at any given moment?

Classification of Trading Systems by Tactics of Making Trades

Trend-Following Trading Systems

For these trading systems, the main goal is to identify the trend and recognize the signs of the end of a correction. They widely use tools and patterns of technical and wave analysis, trend-following indicators, and oscillators. Trades are mainly made "from pullbacks".

Pros:
  • Large profit range; relatively small stops;
  • The trading system allows confidently increasing open positions.
Cons:
  • The trader usually doesn't immediately recognize a trend change, so the main losses occur during reversals.

Trading Systems Operating on Breakouts of "Support" and "Resistance" Levels

These trading systems generally place the significance of trend direction in the background. The main rule here is the breakout of "support" and "resistance" levels as the primary signal of the emergence or development of a trend.

Pros:
  • The trading system provides the most reliable signals of trend formation and possible boundaries of its end.
Cons:
  • False breakouts of "support" and "resistance" levels, especially in highly volatile markets, are the main problem of such trading systems.

Trading Systems Operating Inside a Price Corridor

For such a trading system, the main goal is to identify the formation of a sideways flat. Trades here are made from one border of the flat to the other. These trading systems widely use oscillators with "oversold" and "overbought" zones, such as the stochastic oscillator.

Pros:
  • The trading system allows earning where the majority of traders lose.
Cons:
  • It is difficult to timely identify the end of the flat.
  • The trader misses the main price movement range by trend.
