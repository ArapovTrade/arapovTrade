World Stock Indices

Dow Jones Industrial Average

The industrial index of shares of 30 leading US corporations. Sometimes the composition is rotated based on the level of capitalization (amounts of equity). This is probably the most popular index in the world. It includes 30 of the largest US companies from various industries: finance, transportation, consumer, food, and industrial sectors. The index includes well-known companies worldwide: Coca-Cola, IBM, Intel, Microsoft, General Motors, and others.

Standard and Poor's 500 Index (S&P 500)

A capitalization-weighted index calculated from the shares of 500 leading US corporations. Therefore, the index itself can be used to judge the state of the country's economy, as it covers all major sectors of the country.

NASDAQ-100

An index of over-the-counter stocks. The shares are mainly of companies in the high-tech sector.

FTSE 100

An index of the top 100 corporations in the UK. It is the most respected and quoted index on European platforms. It includes the 100 largest companies traded on the London Stock Exchange.

Nikkei 225

A Japanese index that includes 225 companies. The composition is reviewed annually. It includes giants such as Honda, Panasonic, Mazda, and others. With a probability of 99.9%, all Japanese brands you know are included in the Nikkei 225. Similar to the S&P 500, it quite objectively reflects the state of the economy. It is the most important index in the Asian region.

DAX

A German stock index that includes the 30 most important companies in the country: Adidas, BMW, Henkel, Volkswagen, and others.
