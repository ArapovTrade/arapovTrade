Trader's Trading Plan

Helping a trader effectively execute their activities involves maintaining a trading plan. A trading plan should be a mandatory element for every trader. Developing a trading plan disciplines the trader and helps them not deviate from their trading strategies in stressful situations.

A trading plan should outline the following aspects:

  • Profit and loss limits: The trader must control their emotions and avoid drawdowns beyond set limits. At the same time, it's essential to have a clear goal and the ability to take profits in a timely manner.
  • Entry and exit conditions: It is necessary to determine in advance under what conditions the trader will apply certain entry and exit strategies.
  • Key events of the trading day: Determining the release date of important news for the traded instruments to be ready for market reactions at the time of their publication.
  • Details: It is necessary to specify the instruments used, open charts, and applied timeframes. It is also worth noting the probable instruments with which deals may be concluded based on correlation.
  • Trading conditions: Determining the conditions under which the trader stops trading, such as changes in volatility, unexpected price movements, or deterioration of the psychological state.

It is recommended to prepare reports on past trades at the end of the trading day to regularly record the main trading indicators. This will help assess the effectiveness of the chosen trading strategy in the future.
