Economic State of the Country

Labor Market Data:

  • Non-Farm Payrolls - The number of newly created jobs in all sectors of the economy except agriculture. For the US, it shows the true picture of employment. Growth in NFP leads to currency appreciation. Released monthly on Fridays - the days of the largest movements due to the completion of the weekly currency movement cycle.
  • Average Earnings - The average hourly wage rate (Increase in the indicator - increase in inflation expectations).
  • Unemployment - The percentage of unemployed people out of the total working-age population. Rising unemployment - cooling of the labor market; currency depreciation.
  • Jobless Claims - The number of people applying for unemployment benefits (released weekly).
  • Claimant Count - The UK equivalent of NFP and Unemployment.
  • Unit Wage - The UK equivalent of Average Earnings.

Inflation Indicators:

  • Consumer Price Index - Consumer (retail) price index. An indicator of existing inflation. Rising inflation amid economic growth leads to currency appreciation as an increase in the interest rate is expected.
  • Money Supply (M0-M4) - Monetary aggregates.
  • Producer Price Index - Producer price index (wholesale prices). An index of emerging inflation that exists only at the stage of raw materials and components entering the production process. Divided into INPUT and OUTPUT.
  • Employment Cost Index - The cost of a unit of labor.
