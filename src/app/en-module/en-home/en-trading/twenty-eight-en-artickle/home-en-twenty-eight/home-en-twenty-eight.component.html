Key Price Patterns in Technical Analysis

Many traders have probably heard of the term "price action" or analysis using Japanese candlesticks. However, the graphical method of forecasting is very rich and offers an alternative to the types of analysis mentioned above. It involves concepts such as trend continuation and reversal patterns.

Trend Continuation Patterns

Trend continuation patterns primarily include the flag and pennant. These models are fairly easy to identify on a FOREX chart. Their distinguishing feature is that they appear after quite strong impulses in one direction.

For those unfamiliar with trend continuation patterns, such as the flag, they might seem like traps. Many novice traders often fall into these traps on FOREX, mistaking the flag for a trend reversal signal. In reality, the flag indicates a temporary market consolidation and preparation for a trend continuation.

Trend Reversal Patterns

The choice of "price action" patterns in this category is much more diverse. These include various wedges, head and shoulders, double/triple bottoms/tops. All these models share that during their formation, the trader already has an idea of the further market movement. Trend reversal patterns on the FOREX market indicate possible price corrections in the near future.

What is important to remember when working with such patterns? Firstly, that they have their own formation rules. If the FOREX trader follows them carefully, success awaits. However, deviation from them can cause significant failures and losses. Moreover, professionals do not recommend trading within the patterns, preferring to wait for clear signals of their completion and the beginning of their development.

What are the advantages and disadvantages of using such patterns in work? The advantages include, for example, that they allow working in various market conditions on FOREX. This is effectively an alternative that allows creating your own trading strategies on FOREX, relying only on your strengths. Another advantage of patterns is that they appear on different time frames and can be used with any financial instruments. Regardless of the asset the trader uses, graphical analysis on FOREX allows finding profitable entry points.

On the other hand, this method of forecasting price fluctuations has significant disadvantages. One of the main ones is subjectivity in chart evaluation. The fact is that patterns almost never appear in their classic form, leading to disagreements. Two traders looking at the same chart can see completely different pictures. Besides subjectivity, there is also the factor of waiting. Not everyone will wait until the pattern is fully formed to make a decision. Many traders start working within the models, which can result in very negative consequences. Until the pattern gives a final signal, the trader simply cannot know if it will actually develop. There are known cases when patterns "broke" at the final stage of their formation.
