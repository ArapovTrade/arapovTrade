Requotes in Trading

Introduction

Requotes occur when brokers suggest a different price for a trade after a trader submits an order. This situation is common in financial markets, particularly during high volatility. While requotes are a natural part of trading mechanisms, they often frustrate traders.

This article explains what requotes are, their causes, how to minimize their occurrence, and how to handle them effectively to maintain an efficient trading strategy.

What Are Requotes?

Requotes are a broker's repeated offer of a price after a trader's initial request for execution. This means the initial price is no longer available, and the broker proposes a new price for the trade. Requotes commonly happen in markets with high volatility, where prices can change within fractions of a second.

Key Characteristics of Requotes:

  • Cause: Prices changed before the order could be executed.
  • Consequence: Delayed trade execution or missed trading opportunities.
  • Frequency: Depends on the broker's quality and market conditions.

Although requotes can be frustrating, it is important to understand that they are not always a sign of broker dishonesty. Often, they are a result of market dynamics.

Reasons for Requotes

There are several factors that can lead to requotes:

1. High Volatility

During important news events or the release of economic data, the market can move at a very high speed. This makes the price available a few seconds ago invalid, and the broker is forced to offer a new price.

2. Order Execution Delays

Delays in order processing on the broker's side or in the trader's internet connection can result in the price changing before the order is executed.

3. Type of Order Execution

Different brokers use different order execution models. For example, brokers with a Dealing Desk (DD) model are more likely to experience requotes because they process trades manually, unlike brokers with a Non-Dealing Desk (NDD) model.

4. Lack of Liquidity

In markets with low liquidity, the price can change quickly due to a lack of sufficient buyers or sellers. This can also result in requotes.

How Do Requotes Affect Trading?

Requotes can significantly impact trading results and the overall experience of working with a broker. Here are the main consequences:

1. Missed Opportunities

When a broker offers a new price, the trader may decline the trade if the price does not meet their expectations. This can result in missing out on profitable trading opportunities.

2. Reduced Profit

If the new price is less favorable than the requested one, it can decrease the trader's potential profit. For instance, when opening a trade with a planned profit, a price change may reduce this profit.

3. Psychological Pressure

Frequent requotes can cause frustration and affect the trader's emotional state, leading to impulsive decisions.

4. Increased Execution Time

Requotes slow down the trading process, which is particularly critical in highly volatile markets where time is of the essence.

How to Avoid Requotes?

Although it is impossible to completely avoid requotes, there are several strategies that can help minimize their frequency:

1. Choose a Reliable Broker

Look for brokers with a minimal number of requotes. Trader reviews and ratings can help evaluate the quality of order execution.

2. Use Pending Orders

Pending orders allow you to set a price in advance at which the trade should be executed. This helps avoid execution at an unexpected price.

3. Improve Internet Connection

High-speed internet connections reduce delays and minimize the risk of requotes caused by technical issues.

4. Avoid Trading During News Events

During the release of significant news, the market becomes extremely volatile, increasing the likelihood of requotes. Avoid trading during these times.

5. Choose the Right Trading Time

Trade during the most liquid sessions (e.g., during the overlap of the European and American sessions) to reduce the risk of requotes.

The Role of the Broker in Requotes

The broker plays a key role in the frequency and impact of requotes. Traders need to understand how brokers operate and what conditions they offer to choose the right partner for trading.

1. Execution Model

Brokers with a Dealing Desk (DD) often provide requotes as they act as counter-parties in trades. NDD brokers (STP or ECN) typically pass trades directly to the market, reducing the likelihood of requotes.

2. Liquidity Quality

Brokers with access to high-quality liquidity providers can offer more stable prices, reducing the risk of requotes.

3. Regulation

Regulated brokers typically provide more transparent trading conditions and encounter requotes less frequently.

4. Technical Infrastructure

Modern servers and technologies allow brokers to minimize delays in order execution, which also reduces the likelihood of requotes.

How to Deal with Requotes?

If you encounter requotes, it's essential to learn how to handle them effectively:

1. Accepting the New Price

If the proposed price aligns with your strategy, you can accept it and proceed with the trade. It's important to establish an acceptable price deviation range beforehand.

2. Waiting for the Right Moment

If the new price doesn't meet your expectations, reject the trade and wait for a better opportunity to enter the market.

3. Analyzing Market Conditions

Regularly analyze the market to predict periods of high volatility and avoid them when possible.

4. Using Automated Systems

Trading robots and algorithms can help minimize human error and respond more quickly to market changes.

Conclusion

Requotes are an inevitable part of trading on financial markets, especially during periods of high volatility. However, by understanding their causes and employing effective strategies, traders can minimize their impact on trading results.

Choosing a reliable broker, using pending orders, developing risk management skills, and analyzing market conditions will help you handle requotes. With the right approach, you can maintain the effectiveness of your trading strategy and move closer to achieving your financial goals.

Remember, trading requires discipline, patience, and continuous learning. Use the knowledge gained to improve your results and confidently step towards success!
