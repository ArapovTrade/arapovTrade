How to Choose a Timeframe for Trading on the Stock Exchange

The choice of trading period depends on several conditions, but the first and foremost is the trading strategy of a particular trader, of which there are several types:

Scalping:

A strategy focused on making a large number of quick trades, with the closing period ranging from a few seconds to a few minutes. The strategy involves using the shortest timeframes: one-minute (M1) and five-minute (M5).

Intraday, or day trading

This strategy involves making trades within a single trading day, although their number is significantly less compared to scalping. The duration of one trade ranges from a few minutes to a few hours. Traders who use this strategy usually use timeframes from five minutes (M5) to one hour (H1).

Medium-term strategy

This approach involves making longer trades, from one day to a week. Traders who choose this strategy most often analyze charts with timeframes from one hour (H1) to one day (D1).

Long-term trading

The least intensive but long-lasting and potentially profitable strategy, which is more like investing than active trading. Trades within the long-term strategy usually last at least a week or longer. In this case, large timeframes are used, starting from one day (D1) to several months (MN).

It is important to understand that by limiting the analysis to one or two timeframes, the trader misses a lot of information for analysis. Charts on different timeframes can show different price behavior. For example, an analysis on a five-minute chart may show "bullish" signals for opening a long position. However, technical analysis on an hourly or longer timeframe may indicate the approach of the instrument to a local price maximum, which may be followed by a pullback.
