Separation of trading systems. Duration of the transaction

Intraday Trading Systems

Trades usually last no more than one day. A prominent example of this approach is "scalping".

Pros:
  • Short stops.
  • A large deposit is not necessary to start trading.
  • A greater potential for profit compared to other approaches.
  • More precise execution of individual movements.
Cons:
  • More stressful work â€” the trader is mostly constantly near the trading terminal, making it difficult to plan their time.
  • Trades with good medium-term or long-term potential are closed at the end of the day in the hope of re-entering the same direction tomorrow.
  • A large number of trades can bring not only more profit but also increased risks.

Medium-Term Trading Systems

Trades last from several days to several weeks.

Pros:
  • Work is conducted at a more relaxed pace.
  • The trader is less tied to the computer.
  • This approach allows the trader to pursue other activities.
Cons:
  • Increased stop sizes.
  • Market reactions to important news can instantly nullify the trader's work over several days.
  • Average earnings are generally lower than those of short-term traders.

Long-Term Trading Systems

Trades last from several weeks to several months. The trader essentially turns from a speculator into an investor.

Pros:
  • The trader has enough time to analyze the market and make trading decisions.
  • The trader can fully dedicate themselves to other activities.
Cons:
  • A relatively large deposit is required.
