Analysis of the FOREX Market

Working in the FOREX market means the need to stay constantly updated on business events. The subject of fundamental analysis includes the following factors:

  • economic indicators published by information agencies and statistical bodies in the form of reports on absolute indicators and indexes. The time of announcement of these indicators is always known in advance and indicated in special economic calendars, so they belong to the group of expected factors. Of particular importance are indicators of the US economy, inflation, and unemployment levels;
  • news and events, which are divided into expected or planned (economic, political news) and unexpected or random (political, natural, force majeure events). Expected news has a strong impact on the market even before it appears, as the rule "buy on rumors, sell on facts" works. Unexpected news has a short life cycle but impacts the market much more strongly as it is not anticipated.

There are two life cycle options for fundamental factors:

  • a short life cycle ranging from one day to several days, typical of unexpected news and rumors;
  • a long life cycle lasting from several weeks to several years, characteristic of general macroeconomic indicators and political news.

Rumors cause a chain reaction among market participants — everyone rushes to make a deal. When a rumor appears, a new powerful wave emerges in the market, which acts until the rumor is confirmed or refuted — after this, the reverse movement of the exchange rate occurs, as the main thing is to "jump off the train" before the new trend starts. The most common rumor is the expectation of a change in the discount rate. The expectation of rising interest rates means an increase in the national currency rate.

The impact of fundamental factors causes one of the possible market reactions upon announcement:

  • if market expectations are generally met, there will be no significant change in rate dynamics;
  • if market expectations are not met because the factor was underestimated, the current trend will continue with a possible acceleration at the moment the news appears;
  • if market expectations are fundamentally wrong, a reversal of the market direction is possible. As a rule, when news appears, the market freezes for a while, understanding what is happening. During this time, major market participants "hold" the rate to have time to close previously opened positions that have become unprofitable and open positions in the other direction;
  • if fundamental news contradicts the current trend, its impact on the market will be short-term;
  • if fundamental news confirms the current trend, the market movement accelerates, but the likelihood of a rollback increases.
