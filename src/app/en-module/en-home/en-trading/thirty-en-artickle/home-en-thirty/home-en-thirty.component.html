Types and Kinds of Orders

Classification

To open and close positions, exchange orders (orders) are used. By type of execution, they distinguish between market orders (market order) and pending orders (pending order). Pending orders, in turn, can be limit (limit), stop (stop), or stop-limit (stop limit). An order of each type can be used to buy (buy) or sell (sell).

Passive Limit Order

A limit order is an order to buy or sell a certain amount of an asset at the specified price or better. For now, we consider regular (passive) limit orders.

A buy limit order is an order to buy at the specified price or lower (as it is profitable for the buyer to have the price as low as possible). The buy limit order is placed at a price lower than the current market price. The trader uses this order when he hopes that the price will fall first, but then, reaching the set level, will turn around and start to rise (i.e., he expects a rebound from the support level).

A sell limit order is an order to sell at the specified price or higher (as it is profitable for the seller to have the price as high as possible). The sell limit order is placed at a price higher than the current market price. The trader uses this order when he hopes that the price will rise first, but then, reaching the set level, will turn around and start to fall (i.e., he expects a rebound from the resistance level).

To execute a limit order, there must be an opposite market order.

The limit order is used both to open a position and to close it with a profit: a buy limit order provides an exit from a short position (sale), and a sell limit order provides an exit from a long position (purchase).

Participants in trades using limit orders provide liquidity, i.e., they allow other participants to make transactions by agreeing to the prices indicated in these limit orders.

If we compare the exchange with a vegetable market, participants who have placed limit orders are like traders who have laid out their goods on the counters and set price tags (with the only exception that on the market goods are usually only sold, and on the exchange, trading instruments are both sold and bought).

Order Book

All limit orders sent to the exchange by all trading participants form the so-called order book (order book).

The price at which the last transaction on the exchange took place is called the Last price. Above the Last price, limit sell orders are placed in the order book. Below are limit buy orders. A limit sell order at the best price (closest to the Last price) is called an offer (Offer) or ask (Ask).

A limit buy order at the best price (closest to the Last price) is called a bid (Bid).

The gap between the bid and ask is called the spread.
