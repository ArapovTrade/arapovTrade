Major central banks

FRS (Federal Reserve System)

The Federal Reserve System: The Central Bank of the USA consists of federal reserve banks of the states. It was created in 1913. It controls monetary circulation and implements monetary policy in the USA.

FOMC (Federal Open Market Committee)

The Federal Open Market Committee. The committee meets once every 4-6 weeks and makes decisions on open market operations and changes in the discount rate.

BOE (Bank of England)

The Bank of England. It meets on the first Thursday of each month.

MPC (Monetary Policy Committee)

The Monetary Policy Committee of the Bank of England. 9 members of the committee vote on changes to the discount rate during the meeting.

ECB (European Central Bank)

The European Central Bank, led by Mario Draghi.

BOJ (Bank of Japan)

The Bank of Japan. Led by Haruhiko Kuroda.

Bundesbank (Bundesbank)

The Bundesbank of Germany. Led by Ernst Welteke.

SNB (Swiss National Bank)

The Swiss National Bank - the quietest and most discreet in the market. Led by Thomas Jordan.

Each regional FOREX market has its own characteristics. Asian market - FOREX trading hours

During this trading session, the most active transactions are in the exchange operations market of the US dollar to the yen (USD/JPY), the euro to the yen (EUR/JPY), the euro to the dollar (EUR/USD), and the Australian dollar to the US dollar (AUD/USD).

European market - FOREX trading hours

The trading session schedule in Europe is quite different from the Asian sessions due to time zone differences. Frankfurt opens at 8:00 Central European Time, and London at 9:00. At this time, trading takes place in European financial centers. The volatility of the most popular currency pairs increases significantly after the start of the FOREX trading session on the London market. Trading activity decreases somewhat by lunchtime, but in the evening, traders are again actively trading. Changes in exchange rates during the European trading session can be substantial, as the bulk of financial assets are concentrated in Europe.

American market - FOREX trading hours

The most active operations on FOREX begin with the opening of the New York session. At this time, Forex trading begins for US banks, and European dealers return from lunch breaks. The influence of European and American banks is approximately equal, so no significant changes compared to the European trading session occur on Forex. However, after the European market closes, volatility may increase. This is often observed on Friday evenings before the weekend. The American session is more aggressive in trading than others.
