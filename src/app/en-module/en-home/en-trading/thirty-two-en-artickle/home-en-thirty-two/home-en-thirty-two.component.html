Stop Order

A stop order is an order to buy or sell a certain amount of an asset at the specified price or worse.

A buy stop order is an order to buy at the specified price or higher. The trader uses this order when hoping that the price, in its upward movement, reaching the set level, will continue to rise. The buy stop order is placed at a price higher than the current market price. On the stock market, as soon as the last trade price (Last) equals or exceeds the price set in the buy stop order, it immediately turns into a market order to buy and is executed under the same conditions as all other market orders. On FOREX, for a buy stop order to trigger, the Ask price must be equal to or greater than the price specified in the order.

A sell stop order is an order to sell at the specified price or lower. The trader uses this order when hoping that the price, in its downward movement, reaching the set level, will continue to fall. The sell stop order is placed at a price lower than the current market price. On the stock market, as soon as the last trade price (Last) equals or falls below the price set in the sell stop order, it immediately turns into a market order to sell and is executed under the same conditions as all other market orders. On FOREX, for a sell stop order to trigger, the Bid price must be equal to or lower than the price specified in the order.

A stop order is used both to open a position and to exit it. In the latter case, the stop order is used as a protective order, limiting losses (stop loss): a buy stop order provides protection for a short position (sale), and a sell stop order provides protection for a long position (purchase).
