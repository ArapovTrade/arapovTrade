Beginner Trader Mistakes

One of the main mistakes beginners make, often having no understanding of risk management, is refusing to limit losses. Many hold out hope for a price reversal until the last moment, which often leads to massive losses. The price may eventually return to the trade's opening point, but will the trader have enough capital to survive such losses?

This leads to the primary rule beginners should grasp: never trade without stop orders. Often, novice traders forego risk management and even stop orders in favor of high-risk strategies, on which a few professional traders once made a big score.

However, it's crucial to understand that using such strategies even for experienced players is a huge risk. For beginners, these strategies are nearly suicidal since their implementation is almost impossible without experience and a steady hand. Relying on luck in stock trading is the quickest path to failure.
