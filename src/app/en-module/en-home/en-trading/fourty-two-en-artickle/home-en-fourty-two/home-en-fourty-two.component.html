Basic timeframes in trading

Timeframe is the time interval of quotes displayed on the price chart. When switching from one timeframe to another, the "volume" of one candle or bar changes - the time that a single bar represents.

Simply put, a timeframe is the period of each candle or bar. For example, if you have a minute timeframe (M1), each candle on the chart will equal an interval of 1 minute. If the timeframe is daily (D1), each candle will equal 1 calendar day.

In fact, by changing the timeframe, a trader gains the ability to analyze the dynamics of a financial instrument in terms of grouping information over various intervals. The importance of such analysis lies in the need to forecast prices for different periods, depending on the specific trading strategy.
