What is Divergence?

Divergence is a technical term that describes a situation where the price on the chart shows a certain wave, then tries to continue moving in the same direction but cannot, and the market reverses. Why does this happen? Prices are formed through an auction process where participants compete for the price. If there is a sharp drop with high volumes, it may mean that those who bought earlier at higher prices are trying to get rid of their positions due to losses.

How to Avoid Traps?

To avoid traps that beginners fall into, I suggest a safe trading method. It is important to always monitor divergence. When there is an active price drop with high volumes indicating strong selling, you need to wait for the moment when the price tries to go below the previous low with decreasing volumes. If at this moment volumes do not increase (on the contrary, they decrease), this is the first sign of divergence. The price decreases, but volumes do not grow.

Usually, in such situations, when the price goes down, volumes should increase to confirm the continuation of the trend. If this does not happen, it may indicate that sellers have exhausted their resources, and buyers are starting to take control.

Example

For example, after a strong price drop with high volumes, we see an attempt by the market to drop below the previous low again, but volumes do not increase. This is a signal that the strength of the sellers is exhausted, and the market may start to rise.
