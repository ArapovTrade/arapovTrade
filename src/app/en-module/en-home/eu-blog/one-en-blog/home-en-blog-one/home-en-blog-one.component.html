Market phases in trading

Hello, friends! I'm Igor Arapov, and today I will talk about the key phases of the market. This topic is very important for those who want to become successful traders.

So, the main phases of the market:

  • trend;
  • balance;
  • trend change.

Every asset goes through these phases, and your task is to timely determine which phase you are in. One of the most important rules you should remember is that the market is constantly changing.

Impulse movements are followed by periods of calm when the market digests information. Even if the market is actively moving, there will come a moment of consolidation â€” a sideways movement that can last for a long time. These are the main elements of market movement.

For example, American writer, stock, and commodity trader Larry Williams in his book "Long-Term Secrets to Short-Term Trading" highlights two main patterns: expansion and contraction.

The market cannot move in one direction forever or stay in place. The main constants of any market are contraction and expansion. Expansion is a trending movement, and the trader's task is to recognize it in time and join in.

The key skill of a trader is to determine the phase of the cycle. Before the start of a trend, there are signs that help to identify it. And now I will talk about the balance phase of the market, which needs to be understood and mastered as it precedes the trend phase. Successful traders follow the trend.

Comfortable trading is following the trend. Understanding how a trend is born allows you not only to enter a new trend in time but also to protect yourself from working against it. This helps avoid large losses that often occur when trading against the trend.

The market forgives many mistakes, but there will come a moment when it will punish for them. Trading against the trend is one of the critical mistakes that should never be made. We will analyze the trend change using the Smart Money concept and volume analysis. Volume analysis helps determine trends and understand what is happening in the market.

The market is a non-linear system, and a trader needs to be attentive and focused. Living in a changing environment is difficult, and the market is exactly that kind of environment.
