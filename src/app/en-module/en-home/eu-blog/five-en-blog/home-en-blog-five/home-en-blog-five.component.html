Pricing and Liquidity

Hello everyone! Today I will talk about how prices are formed in the market and what liquidity problems large players face.

To begin with

The price in any market is determined by two indicators: the bid price and the ask price (or offer). The bid price shows how much buyers are willing to pay, while the ask price shows how much sellers are willing to sell without affecting the price. We are currently considering an example with Ethereum.

The higher the price, the more liquidity in the market, and the lower the price, the more buyers are willing to buy.

Now I want to define a very important term:

Spread is a very important component of supply and demand. The difference between buyers and sellers, defined as the spread, is usually a criterion of liquidity. The wider the spread, the harder it is to execute trades in the market, and the narrower the spread, the easier it is for buyers and sellers to find their counterparties.

From this, we can conclude: if the spread, the difference between bid and ask, increases, it becomes harder to execute trades in the market. The smaller the spread, the easier it is for buyers and sellers to find each other.

If we stretch the spread wider and build it along the market extremes, we will see that there is a price where sellers are actively selling and a price where buyers are actively buying. Problems for large players arise when they consider an asset very cheap and want to buy, but liquidity runs out, and they have to buy at market prices. Conversely, when the asset is expensive, they want to sell, but there is no one to sell to, and the price falls.

The price in the market moves from one level to another, from cheap to expensive and vice versa.

Market Manipulations

Market manipulations are often related to finding liquidity. Large players who bought coins at a low price want to sell them for a profit. But the volumes they have accumulated are hard to realize because there are no buyers at high levels. Manipulations look like sharp price movements, often during news events. This is done to draw small traders into the market and use their liquidity to fix profits. After this, the market often becomes bearish.

This point needs to be understood when trading levels, especially in markets with extremely low liquidity. Now, in the bear market, we can see that the Ethereum market is very unclaimed, the demand for it is very low. Those who buy at the lows face great difficulties in realizing their profits. Therefore, before selling, it is advisable to wait for manipulation by those who are holding buys. When they realize their profits and the market shows that demand is flooded, it can be confidently shorted. This is just a hypothetical example that can arise in one of these markets.
