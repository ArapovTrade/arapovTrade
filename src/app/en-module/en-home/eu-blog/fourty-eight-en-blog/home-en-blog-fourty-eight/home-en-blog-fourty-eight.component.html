Instructions for self-study in Trading

Hello! My name is Igor Arapov. I have been trading for over 11 years. In this video, I want to help you master the profession of a trader. If you are self-taught, like many beginners, it often ends in failure. The problem is that without a clear structure, the information turns into a mess, and you won't know how to trade. It is best to have a trading system. With it, any trader knows when to buy and sell. I will explain the steps you need to take to achieve this goal.

The first thing you need to do is familiarize yourself with the basics of the market. Learn the terms to understand what traders are talking about. This can be done using a glossary of terms. Then get to know the exchange: what it is, what is traded on it, who its participants are, and how it is regulated. This is important for general understanding.

Volume analysis of the market is the basis of my approach to trading. To confidently read volumes, you need to trust the information received. Any normal exchange has clearing, controlling the fairness of trades. Volumes are not just graphs at the bottom of the screen but important information. Study it to be confident in your actions.

Learning should be built from simple to complex

Market basics are the foundation. You can't skip these topics, or you will have to make them up later. The goal of learning is to have a clear idea of where and when to open trades. It all starts with the basics, which need to be mastered quickly and easily.

Next, study the types of market analysis. Fundamental analysis can be skipped as it is complex and requires a deep understanding of macroeconomics. Technical analysis is your field. This is where trading begins: types of charts, types of trends, main patterns. They need to be known but not necessarily sought out. Volume analysis is what I teach. Here you need to learn to read volumes. Big capital sells on the rise and buys on the fall. Understanding this, you can profit from an inefficient market.

Types of orders

It is also important to understand the types and kinds of orders. I have a video on this topic on the channel. Understanding limit orders and their impact on the market will explain why volume analysis is so important.

After theory, move on to the specific study of volumes. I talk about the trading method, phases of market movement, reasons for trend changes. All this needs to be mastered because it is the basis of your understanding of trading. Pay special attention to priority change levels. This is a technical pattern that works well. Learn the basics of volume in the VSA system, linking price with volume. This will help you use the trading system.

Managing deposit and risks

Managing your deposit and risks, setting stop-losses, calculating position size — this is the basis of money management. Profit is made over the long run, not in one trade. Understanding this will help you trade successfully.

Trading psychology

The section on psychology is also very important. When you start earning, greed comes, which ruins many traders. Read and master this section to avoid mistakes.

By studying all these sections, you will be able to trade confidently and not depend on news, rumors, and advice. Set stop-losses — this is the golden rule of trading.
