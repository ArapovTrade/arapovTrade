Imbalance in trading

Hello everyone! My name is Igor Arapov, and today we will talk about market imbalance. This is a popular topic with many videos about it. Imbalance is associated with trend reversal points and how a global reversal occurs. The name speaks for itself: there was balance, and then it was gone. If you want to catch trend reversals, you need to learn to see these moments on the chart.

Finding Balance on the Chart

First, you need to find balance on the chart. This helps to understand how the market transitions from a calm state to an imbalance, that is, to a trend. You need to see a flat â€” this is when orders and volumes are concentrated in one range. It is important that one of the participants runs out in this range.

The basis of any bullish trend is a supply deficit. Smart participants buy on declines, understanding the value. But sooner or later, sellers run out in their range, and they have to raise the price to buy. This is the logic of imbalance: when volumes in the range are high, you can buy with limit orders without affecting the price. But when volumes run out, large buyers have to raise the price to get scarce liquidity. This leads to a trend change.

Imbalance is usually a reversal pattern, and it is important to look for it at levels. At value levels, the accumulation phase occurs, after which the trend changes.
