The whole truth about futures trading

Hello, friends! My name is Igor Arapov and today I will tell you about futures.

Futures are an important part of trading, and anyone who wants to trade needs to know what they are and how they work. This will help save money when trading. A future is a contract that obliges you to fulfill the conditions. I want to emphasize the word "obliges". This is a contract that obliges you on the exchange.

What are Futures?

Futures appeared as a way to fix the price for the seller, for example, so that a farmer could fix the price of wheat in advance. With the development of the market, settlement futures appeared, which do not imply the delivery of goods. It's a game for money: you earn if the price goes in your direction, and you lose if it goes against you.

Futures are a risky instrument due to high volatility. Especially in cryptocurrency markets, where there is a lot of speculative money. This money comes to the market and is redistributed.

Obligations in Futures Contracts

A futures contract obliges you to perform certain actions. For example, if you bought bitcoin at 71,000 and the price fell to 50,000, you would lose 28%. This happens due to the work of market makers who provide liquidity in the market. If the price goes down, they can remove support, and you will lose money.

In the spot market (e.g., buying stocks), no one forces you to sell. You can hold them for a long time. But in futures, the market can collapse, and you will lose money.

Risks and Benefits

When you sell a future, you are obliged to return it at a higher price if the price rises. This benefits market makers who make money on your losses. This is a brutal business, and it needs to be understood to avoid losing money.
