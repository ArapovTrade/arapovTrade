Candlestick patterns in Price Action

Hello everyone! My name is Igor Arapov. Today, I want to talk about Price Action patterns, specifically about bullish and bearish engulfing.

This article is intended for beginners, as technical analysis “Price Action” is very popular among novice traders. Unlike fundamental analysis or “Smart Money” analysis, most beginners start with “Price Action”. Therefore, in this video, we will discuss the pros and cons of “Price Action”, why I don't use it, and what beginners need to understand.

To start with, “Price Action” is about price patterns. We need to find certain formations on the chart and use them. Using the euro-dollar example, I drew two levels. These levels form a range. At the lower boundary, there was a bullish engulfing pattern three times.

A “bullish engulfing” pattern consists of two bars. The first bar is bearish, and the second bar is bullish, fully covering the body of the first bar. For example, a small down bar followed by a bullish candle with a higher close. This is a bullish engulfing.

A “bearish engulfing” is the opposite: a bullish candle followed by a bearish candle that closes lower. These patterns by themselves mean little because within the range, you can see both bullish and bearish engulfing. If you react to every pattern, you might get tired of constantly exiting and re-entering positions.

However, these patterns matter at levels. Why? In volume analysis, I have already mentioned that volume is a reaction to price. Participants see ranges and levels where the price cannot break through several times. This creates a desire to buy at these levels because they consider this price to be the cheapest on the market.

A “bullish engulfing” pattern at a level can lead to a strong bullish movement because there is participant interest, and orders are executed. The price bounces off the level where there is no supply, meaning buyers are forced to push the price higher.

This is an example of the logic behind “Price Action” that you need to use and understand. But don't use it in isolation from levels; otherwise, there will be significant problems.
