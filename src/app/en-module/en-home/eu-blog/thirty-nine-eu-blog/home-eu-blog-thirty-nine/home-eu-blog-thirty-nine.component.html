Volumetric Market Analysis

Hello everyone! Today we will talk about volume analysis of markets. The method of volume analysis originated in the 1900s when Richard Wyckoff, the founder of this method, worked as an editor for a Wall Street-related newspaper. He noticed that there are "smart money" in the market — those who constantly make money on the stock market, whether in a “bull” or “bear” trend. Wyckoff introduced the concept of "market operator" — a collective term for all these “smart players”. According to his logic, “smart money” operates like wholesalers: they buy goods cheaply in bulk and sell them at retail for a profit.

Wyckoff identified phases in the market: the “accumulation phase”, where market operators actively buy the instrument (whether precious metals, stocks, or currency — it doesn't matter), and the “distribution phase”, where the crowd starts buying what it previously wanted to get rid of. There is a peculiar psychology at work in the market: the crowd acts irrationally, while “smart money” acts rationally.

To understand “volume analysis”, you need to learn to identify the phases. Unfortunately, there are fewer such "pictures" in the market as it becomes more complex. Volumes are often "drawn" to confuse those who track the traces of “smart money”. However, the levels we identify as cheap and expensive still fit into Wyckoff's logic.
