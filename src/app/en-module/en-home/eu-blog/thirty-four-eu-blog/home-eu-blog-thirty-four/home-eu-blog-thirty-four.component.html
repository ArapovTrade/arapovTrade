Today we're going to talk about the importance of a stop-loss as a protective order. I've been trading on the market for over 11 years and I want to share my experience, especially with beginner traders, why it's such an important tool and how to use it.

First, it's important to understand that a stop-loss is your friend. It protects your deposit from losses. The most important thing is to have a stop-loss in every trade. Without it, if the market changes dramatically due to news or actions by the Central Bank, you could find yourself in an unfavorable situation and lose more money than planned. To avoid this, a stop-loss is necessary.

Unfortunately, many beginner traders don't use a stop-loss because they don't know how to set it correctly. The market often hits stops if they are set incorrectly. Beginners see the price fluctuating and place orders randomly, hoping for profit. But when the market goes against them, they are trapped.

A stop-loss is a protection tool, and its installation should be part of the plan. Every trader has a trading strategy that includes a trading plan. If you don't have one, check out my videos on the channel youtube.com/@ArapovTrade. I've recorded many trading systems, including the Smart Money concept. The principle of setting a stop-loss is related to the Smart Money concept, as there are smart participants in the market who manipulate prices.

On the gold chart youtube.com I showed a level or price range. You can see how the price often touches this level, falls below the minimums, there's a large volume of sales, and then a sharp return. This is a false break when smart participants take the stop-losses of traders who have set them incorrectly. To avoid this trap, wait for manipulation at the level and set your stop-loss after that. A false break of the level is a signal to watch the market and prepare for buying. The stop-loss can be set behind a high volume. When professionals have collected all stops, the price will not return there. This is the market's bottom. A false break is the extreme point of the chart, and you can use this pattern for correct stop-loss setting.

If you trade a false break, bought, and the market goes lower, it means you have incorrectly identified the false break, but the stop-loss will protect you from major losses. In our example, it was the market's bottom, and gold went up after the false break. Manipulation knocks out the stops of inexperienced traders, and after that, you can confidently set your stop-loss. To be protected, patience is needed. At a critical level where the price constantly touches, the market awaits this action. After that, the trend begins.

In volume analysis, this action is called "shakeout." Professionals push the price lower to knock out those who bought in that range, and then buy these sales. The trend changes from sideways to a growth phase. The key rule for a stop-loss is to place it where the price will not return. A false break of a key level is a signal. If you have patience, you will wait for inexperienced traders to be knocked out, understand the intentions of large players, and join them.

My recommendation for beginners: stop-losses are very important. You cannot trade without a stop-loss, otherwise, you risk your deposit. The main thing is to set the stop-loss consciously. I provided an example of a conscious stop-loss on the gold chart, where there was manipulation at the level, showing the market's bottom. This was a great opportunity to join a new movement and set a stop-loss that will protect you if something changes. With this approach, you will always have a good risk to reward ratio.
