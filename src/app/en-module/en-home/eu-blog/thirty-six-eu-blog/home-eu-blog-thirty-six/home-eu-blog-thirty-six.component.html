To start with, let me remind you that the Smart Money concept involves searching for "smart money". For newcomers not familiar with cryptocurrencies, I'll briefly explain. In any market, there are professionals â€” these are the large players who buy low and sell high. The task of the retail trader is to see when they are active in the market and to buy or sell along with them. That is, you need to understand how the major market participants act and follow them.

On the Bitcoin chart, I've marked two levels. Today we'll discuss the false breakout. A false breakout confirms that there are large and smart money on the market. It is difficult for large capital to enter the market with big money due to limited liquidity. If they were to simply buy, the price would skyrocket. Therefore, they resort to tricks.

How does it work? They create a false breakout at a key level. They wait for sell orders or stop-losses to accumulate below the level from those who bought. The large capital sees these orders and sells on the market to hook these commands. The stop-losses are triggered, and the large capital forms its position on this volume. Before the price goes up, they try to throw other market participants out and gather the maximum liquidity at a favorable price.

Understanding these processes gives us, retail traders, great opportunities. As long as the large capital has not bought, they can manipulate the market and hit stop-losses. Therefore, we do not buy until we see the manipulation. We wait for a false breakout and only then enter the market when we see that the large capital is ready to rise.

The logic of a false breakout is simple: you get a good entry point with controllable risk. If the price goes down, you exit the trade and avoid losses. Buying at the lower edge of the level, you hold the position until the upper edge. The risk-reward ratio is greater than 1:1, and by repeating such trades, you will make a profit.

Large capital does not always facilitate the trading of retail traders, throwing them out of the market. But we need to know this and use it in our trading.
