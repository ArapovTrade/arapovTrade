The Basics of Trend Channels

So, let's start with the basics. Trend channels show the direction of the trend. Understanding whether the trend is going up or down is very important to avoid making bad trades. We always look at trends on longer timeframes because big players in the market form the price with their money and they see the value of the instrument. If you see a downtrend, as in the example of the Swiss franc, it means that the franc is depreciating against the dollar.

Building Trend Channels

Now let's figure out how to build trend channels. If we want to build a bearish trend (when prices are falling), we need to find the highest point on the chart and the first lower high. Draw a line between these two points, then find the first low and draw the support line. The result is a channel where the upper line is the resistance level (a good point for selling), and the lower line is the support level (a place to take profit). Advantages of Trend Channels

Trend channels help traders of all experience levels choose and secure trades. Trading with the trend is always profitable because for every dollar of risk, you get at least two to three dollars of profit. Therefore, it is important to understand the trend and build channels correctly.

Levels and Trend Channels

You can go further and add levels to the channels. For example, to build a new uptrend (when prices are rising), you need to draw a line through the low and the first higher low, and draw the resistance level at the high between them. Now we have a new uptrend channel where the lower line is the support level (a good place to buy), and the upper line is the resistance level (a good place to sell).

Conclusion

Using trend channels will protect you from hasty actions. The most important thing is to build them on high timeframes to understand the opinion of the major market participants who form the price. Join the professionals and trade with the trend.
