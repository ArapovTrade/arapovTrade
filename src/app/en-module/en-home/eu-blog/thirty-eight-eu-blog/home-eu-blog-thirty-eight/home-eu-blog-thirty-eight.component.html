We have a futures chart on gold. We have a good situation for buying, and I will explain in detail why it can be considered that gold is reversing and why here it is worth buying, not selling. The basis of any earnings in the market in the form of speculation is buying with the calculation that the price will go up. The logic is simple: buying gold at 2300 and selling at 2330, we pocket the price difference.

Now the question arises: how to buy so that the price goes up, not down? The problem with many traders is trading at levels where the market often likes to saw back and forth. Let's discuss why this happens. Levels in technical analysis show where it was expensive and where cheap. But sometimes a level may not work. This is explained by volumes that show the true intentions of market participants.

On the internet, the logic of capturing liquidity is actively promoted. It is a justified term because it speaks of the intentions of the participants. In chess, for example, you need to learn to read the intentions of your opponent to win. In the market, it's similar: you need to be able to read the intentions of the participants.

Trading is a money game where there are smart and foolish participants. The smart ones take money from the foolish. Therefore, our task is to understand their intentions. In volume analysis, this boils down to looking for large deals. Large participants do not buy directly from the market; they create conditions for capturing liquidity to buy at favorable prices. Volumes show moments when there is a struggle of supply and demand in the market.

When the price approaches a level but there are no volumes, this is a signal of market weakness. A real reversal will occur where large players start buying actively. Manipulations create conditions under which other participants are forced to sell at a loss, while large players buy. This is the capture of liquidity.

For successful trading, it is important to understand: levels, manipulations at levels, and volumes. Levels show where it is expensive and where cheap, manipulations create conditions for entry, and volumes confirm the intentions of large players.
