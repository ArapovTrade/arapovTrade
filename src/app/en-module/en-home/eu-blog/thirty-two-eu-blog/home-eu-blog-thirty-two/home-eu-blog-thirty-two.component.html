What are the risks of free education?

At first glance, self-study seems like an attractive option. The internet is filled with tutorials, articles, and books. However, this approach has its pitfalls, which can slow down your progress or lead to serious mistakes.

1. Inaccurate information

Many free resources contain outdated or incomplete data. This can result in a flawed understanding of the market and, consequently, financial losses. Often, the authors of such materials lack practical experience or publish unverified information, making their advice ineffective, or even harmful.

Free education also fails to cover nuances critical to trading. For example, risk management or trading psychology is rarely addressed in publicly available resources.

2. Lack of practice

Studying theory without applying it in practice rarely yields results. Paid courses usually include practical assignments that help solidify knowledge. Free platforms often lack such opportunities, making the learning process one-sided and less effective.

Practice is the key to successful trading. Without the opportunity to apply learned skills on demo accounts or simulators, traders risk facing the real market unprepared.

3. Lack of motivation

When education is free, it's easy to abandon. Investing in a course creates a psychological commitment to see it through. When you pay for education, it becomes a motivator to work harder, study materials carefully, and apply the acquired knowledge.

Moreover, paid courses often offer guidance and support, which helps maintain motivation throughout the learning process. Free resources do not provide this advantage, making the process less effective.
