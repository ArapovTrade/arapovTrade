Is it worth purchasing trading courses?

Hello everyone! My name is Igor Arapov, and today I want to talk about paid trading education. This topic isn't very popular among traders, but it's important. Let's figure out if it's worth paying for education. I'll present arguments for and against so you can make the right decision.

To begin with, paying for trading education isn't typical for many people, especially beginners. Usually, experienced traders come to me, tired of losses and self-education. They understand that it's easier to come, learn, get a trading strategy, and practice it than to search for it on their own.

When you start learning on your own, you need to find your trading method and experiment. And experiments in the market cost money. I lost 2 years on self-education myself, so I can confidently say: it's not the best way to become a trader. Loss of time is also a loss of money.

If you don't mind losing time, you can try learning on your own. I have a free playlist on my channel called "Trading from Scratch." There, I cover the main topics and provide a full course on technical analysis. This knowledge will help you master the theory of trading.

The difference between me and a beginner trader is that an experienced trader is more stable, reliable, and profitable. In any case, you'll gain your experience. But under mentorship, you'll get the knowledge that's necessary for you. A mentor will explain what to do right and what not.

Paid education is effective; otherwise, it wouldn't cost money. But it's important not to buy education from people who can't show their results. Education should include not only theory but also practice. Studying theory on your own, you can get confused. Often, experienced traders come to me with such a "mess" in their heads that needs to be "sorted out."

Ideally, teach a trader from scratch. Such people become traders faster, they don't have internal barriers. When you're learning, it's important to let go of the knowledge you've invested a lot of effort into if it turns out to be wrong. Success depends not on the amount of knowledge but on its correctness.

On one side of the scale is your time and money, on the other is paid mentorship. You don't need to pay for trading theory; everything is on the internet. When I started, there wasn't such an abundance of information. Now everything is available, and many can learn on their own. But this is more for the most talented, who see the whole picture and can highlight the nuances.

If you're capable of grasping all the theory and highlighting the nuances, you don't need a tutor. But there are few such people. Most people are average in abilities, like me. Life showed me that I couldn't do it on my own. I tried for 2 years but didn't have enough strength and information.

But even with this information, it's not guaranteed that you'll reach your goal. Trading is both a general theoretical base and practical nuances. You need to practice. In my training, there is always practice, and the trader must pass an exam of thirty trades.

The trader makes trades and shows what they've learned because the market is honest. It doesn't forgive mistakes. In lessons, you can say everything is clear, but practice will show everything. Theory is not enough. Theory is good, but until you put it into practice, the result won't be the same.

To sum up, beginner students can try on their own. Young people with energy and desire can try. Students, as a rule, don't have money, so it's fair.

If you're no longer a student and want to earn, treat trading as a business. Go to a practicing person, learn from them, and start working. It's like going to university, getting a degree, and starting to work.
