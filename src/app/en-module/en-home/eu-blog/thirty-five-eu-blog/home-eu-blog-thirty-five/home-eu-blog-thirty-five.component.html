Let's delve into it. Price is the amount of money for which the seller is willing to transfer goods. In the stock market, there's an order book where sellers and buyers meet. Sellers indicate the price at which they are willing to sell goods, and buyers – the price at which they are willing to buy. Between them, there is a gap – the spread, the difference between the buying and selling price. Sellers want to sell at a higher price, buyers want to buy cheaper.

Trading happens through limit orders in the order book. These are the bids of participants who are ready to sell or buy goods at a certain price. Novices often trade with market orders, not considering levels and value, just following the price chart. If the price is rising, they buy, without thinking about the current value, leading to losses. Not understanding levels, beginners buy high and sell low.

Price is just the last transaction completed in the market. For instance, a deal could have been at 122, 123, or 127 – it's just the last price. Professionals trade in large volumes, and their supply and demand shape the price on the chart. Small traders usually do not affect the price.

Now about value. There is intrinsic value and market-perceived value. Intrinsic value is the real worth of goods, while market-perceived value is how the market sees it. For example, Warren Buffett bought shares of companies that were trading below their liquidation value. This means he was buying shares cheaper than their real worth.

An efficient market is where the price accounts for the value, and in such a market, the price usually doesn't move. Professionals work in an inefficient market, where the price does not account for the value. They can buy cheaply what is worth more and make a profit from it.

Professionals know the true value better than beginners because trading is a profession, not just playing with charts. They use market inefficiency by buying cheap and selling expensive, which creates a trend.

A trend shows that the market is undervaluing or overvaluing goods. Arguing with a trend is one of the key mistakes by beginners. Professionals respond to value, not to price, unlike beginner traders. Analysis methods that work are aimed at identifying transactions by professionals who trade in large volumes. They use volume analysis and the Smart Money concept to manipulate the market and make profits.

Earning on the stock market is possible if you understand the nature of trading and follow the smart money. Professionals know how to use the market to their advantage, and it is unnecessary to argue with them. To trade successfully, join the professionals.
