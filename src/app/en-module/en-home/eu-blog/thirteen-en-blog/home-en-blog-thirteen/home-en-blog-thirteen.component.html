Elliott Waves and Fibonacci Levels

Hello everyone! My name is Igor Arapov. Today we will talk about Elliott Waves and Fibonacci Levels. I have been trading for over 11 years and want to share my experience with these tools.

This is not a critique of Elliott Waves and Fibonacci Levels. They are popular, and many traders use them. I am simply sharing my opinion, and perhaps it will help beginner traders understand whether these tools are necessary for them.

Elliott Waves

Let's start with Elliott Waves. According to Elliott's theory, the market moves in waves. There are five waves in the direction of the trend and three against the trend. The stock market is a place where everyone wants to make money, but the market is unpredictable. Technical analysis tries to find patterns to predict market movements.

Elliott Waves attempt to systematize the market through impulsive and corrective waves. Each wave has sub-waves, creating a fractal nature of the market. The problem is that this is subjective. Two traders can draw the waves differently on the same chart.

It is difficult to build a trading system on subjective factors. A trading system should be objective and logical. Therefore, although the concept of Elliott Waves is popular, I have not found practical application for it in 11 years.

Fibonacci Levels

Now about Fibonacci Levels. We use Fibonacci numbers to understand where a correction will end and join the impulse. Fibonacci numbers exist in nature, but the market is not nature. It reflects human behavior, which is not always subject to mathematical calculations.

Levels work because they show the decisions of market participants, while waves and Fibonacci numbers are an attempt to impose one's opinion on the market. I have seen many traders who successfully trade levels, and very few who successfully use Elliott Waves or Fibonacci Levels.
