Flag figure in trading

Hello everyone! My name is Igor Arapov, and today I will tell you about the "flag" pattern. I have previously mentioned that I do not often use patterns because they are not particularly useful. But the "flag" is an interesting pattern in terms of price projection.

What is a "flag"?

First, you need to understand what a "flag" is. It is a pattern that begins with an impulsive price rise. If we look at the volumes, we will see that there are extremely high volumes at the bottom, indicating a supply shortage. Therefore, buyers are forced to raise prices, and then the price enters a long sideways movement. This sideways movement can vary in shape, appearance, and volatility, but the price moves within a certain range. This can last from a day to a week or longer, depending on the timeframe. By the way, I recommend looking for such formations on higher timeframes, from four hours and up. Intraday formations are less effective.

The Importance of Time and Volume

Why? Because time is a valuable resource in the market. During the sideways movement, open interest decreases: the price moves up and down, and market participants place speculative positions. And before exiting the sideways movement, something interesting happens: the continuation of the rise usually coincides in magnitude with the previous impulse. It is difficult to explain, but it happens. You can consider this or not, but I personally take this into account when trading the "flag". I expect at least the same rise up.

False Breakout

The longer the sideways movement lasts, the more likely a false breakout. If the flag reverses upwards, then before that, the price sharply dives down: it knocks out buyers' stops, pulls into shorts those who work on the level breakout, and then sharply reverses and goes up. This formation is rare in the market, but if you see it, it is useful. A false breakout is a manipulation, and it means there are intentions of large participants that can be traced here. We can also understand the approximate range of price movement.

Conclusion

Of all the technical analysis patterns, I like the "flag" the most because it has a logical rationale for how to work with it and approximate targets for the trade.
