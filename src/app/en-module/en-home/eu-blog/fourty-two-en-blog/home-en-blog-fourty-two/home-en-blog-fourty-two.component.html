Risks of cryptocurrencies for beginners

Hello everyone! My name is Igor Arapov and today I want to discuss the risks associated with investing in cryptocurrencies. Many beginners who come into trading, especially in cryptocurrencies, do so through advertising or advice from friends without fully understanding all the risks. This often ends in failure. Today I want to explain these risks. This is not a criticism of cryptocurrencies, but an objective reality that should be considered if you are going to invest your money in this area.

Let's start with the fact that cryptocurrencies are traded on exchanges, but they are not yet protected by law. For government regulators, they remain an uncertain asset. By buying cryptocurrency, you are essentially buying something whose real value is unclear. In classical financial theory, investments are associated with acquiring intrinsic value, which is often simply not present here. Cryptocurrency only has a price.

What happens next? It often happens that prices rise rapidly and then fall just as quickly. Without legal protection and regulation, the market can resemble a casino. Coin issuers can manipulate the market by controlling supply and demand. This creates conditions for rapid price changes and opportunities to make money on this. However, such markets can be dangerous for those who do not understand the full depth of the risks.

When cryptocurrency prices change sharply, the desire to make money can lead to dangerous decisions. People sometimes buy at peaks, hoping for further growth, but ultimately lose money due to a rapid price drop. This highlights the importance of understanding all aspects of risk and the need to know what you are doing before investing in cryptocurrencies.
