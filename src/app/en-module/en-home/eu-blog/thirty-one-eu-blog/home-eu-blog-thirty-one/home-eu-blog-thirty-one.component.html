Levels of Support and Resistance

Hello everyone! My name is Igor Arapov and today we will talk about support and resistance levels: how to build, identify, and most importantly, how to use them.

When you work in a range, remember that the support level is not just a line, but a whole range. The price will move within these ranges. On the chart, I draw two lines: one indicates the level, and the other shows that the price can go beyond it. You can see the detailed chart in the video.

How to Build Levels?

It all depends on the market context. If there was a bullish trend before, the key support is the market minimum after the first deep correction. There was an upward rise, then a correction, and the price rose again. This point will be your key support level from which you will look for buy points.

The resistance level in this case is not defined, because the trend can continue. If there was an upward trend and you do not plan to sell, then it is better to focus on support. Selling is undesirable because it requires a good understanding of false breakouts, which I have covered in my videos.

Smart Money always uses false breakouts to capture liquidity. Levels are the range of prices within which the price moves. They show the current market value. Levels show the approximate range where the price of the asset is considered cheap, and you need to buy in this zone. We look at the dollar index, but it could be any asset â€” oil, Bitcoin, etc.

At the bottom, we have a low cost, and we need to try to buy in this zone. At the top is a high cost, and it is advisable to exit the market here. We buy at support and exit at resistance, but do not sell at resistance. This is important because working against the trend reduces the likelihood of success.

Market Entry Methodology

To enter the market at the support level, I propose the following methodology: at the bottom, you see volumes, and you need to find a seller deficit at the level. Any price level where it is cheap indicates that there will be no supply there, and that is why the price rises from the level. Volumes help to see this. You can see low volumes in the low-cost zone, and these are your buy points. If you see a large volume on the next approach to the level, this is the first sign of a range change.

Volume analysis will help you avoid sharp market movements against you. If you watch the volumes at the levels, you will not have problems when the market moves sharply against you. Always wait for a signal at the level, such as a bearish bar on low volume, and then buy. If there is no such signal, skip this level and look for a new situation. It is advisable to study volume analysis to be able to join a new trend according to the principle of level breakout and test.
