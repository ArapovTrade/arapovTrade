Support and Resistance Levels

Support and resistance levels are key elements of technical analysis used by traders to identify entry and exit points in the market. These levels help evaluate where the price may reverse or continue its movement. In this guide, we will explore how to build levels, determine their significance, and use them effectively in trading.

What Are Support and Resistance Levels?

A support level is a zone where the price finds "support" and begins to rise. A resistance level, on the other hand, represents a barrier where the price encounters sellers and starts to decline. Together, they form the range in which the price moves.

Main Characteristics of Levels

  • Support: A zone where demand exceeds supply, causing the price to rise.
  • Resistance: A zone where supply exceeds demand, causing the price to fall.
  • Range: The space between support and resistance levels where the price moves within a balance.

How to Build Levels?

Building levels is an essential skill for a trader. They help structure the chart and identify key zones for decision-making.

Steps to Build Levels

  1. Identify significant points on the chart where the price sharply changed direction (peaks and troughs).
  2. Draw horizontal lines through these points.
  3. Consider the market context: for an uptrend, focus on support; for a downtrend, focus on resistance.

Market Context

For an uptrend, the key support is located at the low after the first correction. For a downtrend, the main resistance is at the high after a pullback.

How to Use Levels in Trading?

Support and resistance levels are used for entering and exiting trades. They can be applied in combination with other tools, such as level trading or candlestick patterns, to improve forecast accuracy.

Trading in a Range

When trading within a range, it is important to understand that the support level is a zone for buying, and resistance is for taking profits. Selling against the trend can be risky and requires specific expertise.

Entry Methodology

Use volume analysis to confirm the strength of the level. For example:

  • Low volumes at the support zone indicate a lack of supply, making this zone favorable for buying.
  • High volumes during a retest of resistance may indicate weakness among buyers.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Working with Levels

Advantages

  • Easy to build and use.
  • Applicable to any market and timeframe.
  • Improves the accuracy of entry and exit points.

Disadvantages

  • Risk of false breakouts.
  • Subjectivity in determining levels.
  • Requires additional indicators for confirmation.

Practical Recommendations

  • Study historical charts to identify quality levels.
  • Use volumes to confirm the validity of breakouts.
  • Avoid trading against the trend without a clear signal.

Conclusion

Support and resistance levels are the foundation of technical analysis, helping traders make informed market decisions. They allow structuring the chart, identifying key points, and finding the best moments for entering or exiting the market. However, for success, it is essential not only to know how to build levels but also to use additional analysis tools effectively.
