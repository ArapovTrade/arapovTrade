How to identify a market maker

Hello everyone! My name is Igor Arapov and today we will talk about market makers, or limit players in the market.

Who is a Market Maker?

A market maker is a mysterious market participant who often scares traders. Many blame him for their failures because they do not understand what he is doing. Today we will figure out who is to blame for traders' problems and how to solve them.

First of all, I want to say that a market maker is primarily a limit participant in the market. He himself is, of course, a professional market participant simply because he has algorithms, technologies, and money on his side. And, of course, he is no competition for a retail trader. In other words, he is an informed professional market participant. This is the first point. Second, he maintains liquidity in the market by signing a contract with the exchange. This means that he helps stabilize prices, preventing them from fluctuating sharply. This is his main task.

Two Types of Market Participants

There are two types of participants in the market: those who move the price with market orders, and those who place limit orders. A limit order is an order placed at a specific price, waiting for a counter order. Limit orders are usually placed by professionals because they want to open a position at a favorable price. For example, on the euro chart, you can see how the price falls from the limit level. This means that the limit seller held the price from rising. By analyzing the trading volumes, you can understand where the limit players are and how they influence the price.

Traders' Problems

For a trader, it is not so important who is behind the limit orders; the main thing is to understand that these are rational market participants. Traders' problems are often related to the fact that they take irrational actions. For example, they buy at resistance levels and sell at support levels, which leads to losses.

The Role of a Market Maker

A market maker helps stabilize the market, and without him, there would be more volatility. He approaches trading rationally, like any professional. To trade successfully, it is important to learn to understand the levels on the chart where the price is cheap or expensive. This will help make profitable trades.
