How to choose a trading platform for trading

Hello! I'm Igor Arapov, and today I will tell you about trading platforms. I have been trading for 11 years and want to explain what trading platforms are, why they are needed, and how they work. Let's get started!

In the past, if you have seen old movies, clients would call their brokers to buy or sell stocks. Nowadays, it's simpler: we use computers or phones to open trades. Trading platforms are modern tools that allow you to give orders to the broker to buy or sell.

When you trade through a broker, you use their trading platform. Typically, it's a simple terminal with "Buy" and "Sell" buttons. You give the broker a command to debit money from your account and buy or sell an asset. If we are talking about futures, you can make contracts to buy or sell assets even if you don't have them. The functionality is such that the broker executes your orders in real-time, and you are trading on the market, becoming a market participant.

You may see a similar screen with your broker because, as I mentioned, more professional software provides more capabilities. And now we will talk about it in more detail. Companies that are not brokers but are interested in developing these technologies release their own programs. These programs are much more productive and technologically advanced. These programs include not only drawing tools, such as technical analysis, various levels, trend channels, but also programming capabilities. You can add indicators here. Typically, in any such software, there are basic indicators and entire communities that share systems based on indicators.

Specialized software is designed not only for opening or closing trades but also for thoroughly analyzing the market. This is the main difference between such trading platforms. Of course, if you want to trade productively, it is recommended to use professional software rather than simple ones. Often, experienced traders combine them. For example, they analyze the market in professional software and open trades in simple ones, such as in the broker's regular browser window.

Another advantage of professional software is connecting many data providers. For example, by entering euro-dollar, you can see many providers that can provide market quotes. This allows you to choose the best price and trade where it is most advantageous. Having a choice is always an advantage. These programs also provide access to major exchanges, especially the CME futures exchange in Chicago. There, we look at volume data to analyze supply and demand in the world's major financial markets.

In conclusion, professional programs provide more opportunities for analysis and accurate data. Ordinary programs from brokers are also useful but have less functionality. If you want to become a successful trader, I recommend using professional software for market analysis.
