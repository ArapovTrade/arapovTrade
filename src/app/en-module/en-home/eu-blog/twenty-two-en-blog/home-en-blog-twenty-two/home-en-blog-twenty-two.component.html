Price patterns in trading

Hello everyone! My name is Igor Arapov and today we will discuss the main technical patterns, or price patterns, in technical analysis. This topic is very popular and is the foundation of technical analysis, as traders look for formations that have worked in the past. For example, the "head and shoulders" pattern often predicts a trend reversal, and traders tend to sell when they see it.

Why are technical patterns used?

Personally, I do not use technical patterns because they work with the same probability as they do not work. It's just statistics, and in the long run, they do not provide an advantage. But why are they used then? Perhaps because the brain tries to find patterns in chaos. Just as a person distinguishes a tiger in the forest, traders distinguish patterns in the market.

The market is chaotic, and participants make decisions about buying or selling. However, there are also smart participants, and we analyze their trades through volumes. Today we are talking about patterns. If we see price fluctuations and patterns on the chart, it helps structure the chaos and make decisions. For example, a double top shows that buyers cannot push the price higher, and a bearish trend begins to form.

Limitations of patterns

However, patterns do not always work. A Nobel laureate proved that "head and shoulders" is just a picture that does not always reverse the market. But traders see it and tend to sell, expecting a reversal. What is important is not the pictures themselves, but how market participants react to them.

Market sentiment analysis and volume analysis

Market sentiment analysis and volume analysis are much more important because they show what market participants are doing. If they are buying, the price goes up, if they are selling, it goes down. Patterns do not predict the market, they show possible scenarios for its development.

Patterns program us for certain actions. If you see a flag, you expect growth and buy. But this is not a market forecast, just a possible scenario. In practice, I have not found useful applications for technical analysis patterns. But it is worth knowing them because they influence the actions of other market participants.

Using patterns in the Smart Money concept

In the Smart Money concept, these patterns are used to capture liquidity. Large players manipulate the market, creating movements that catch participants' orders. This helps them take advantageous positions, but no more than that.

Advice for beginner traders

My advice to beginner traders: do not focus on patterns, they will not benefit you. Look at more subtle things: not at the pictures themselves, but at what is behind them and what they can lead to.
