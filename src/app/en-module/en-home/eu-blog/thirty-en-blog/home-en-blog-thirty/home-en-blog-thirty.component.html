What Are Cryptocurrencies?

Today's task is to understand what cryptocurrencies are, how they differ from other assets, and how a beginner can start in this market. When talking about digital assets, they can be compared to securities or commodities. Securities have intrinsic value supported by some assets, such as the reputation of a state or its reserves. Money, for example, has stable value due to government backing. Cryptocurrencies, on the other hand, are created in the blockchain system, but their regulation and protection are not yet as reliable as traditional financial assets. This is one of the key disadvantages of this market.

For me, for example, legal and regulatory support provided by stock markets and exchanges where I trade is important. The cryptocurrency market does not yet have such a level of regulation, which can raise certain concerns. But this does not prevent millions of traders from actively working in this market every day.

Emission and Stability of Cryptocurrencies

Cryptocurrencies are issued in their system by their creators and represent various tokens and coins. Many of them are unfamiliar to me, as I am focused on a narrower range of assets such as Bitcoin and Ethereum. Bitcoin is especially interesting due to its deflationary model - the maximum issuance of 21 million coins. This makes it similar to an electronic analogue of gold, where a limited resource creates stability in value.

Comparing it to traditional money, which states can print as needed, Bitcoin is presented as a constant in deflationary conditions. This means that its value is preserved or increases over time, while traditional money can lose its purchasing power due to inflation.

Caution with Projects and Market Analysis

It is important to distinguish cryptocurrencies from scam projects that have no real backing and are created without any limitations. Beginners should be cautious with such projects, which may promise quick and large profits but often turn out to be fraudulent. This can lead to investment losses.

After starting to trade cryptocurrencies, beginners usually realize that the market requires deep understanding and analysis. Volume analysis is especially important in the cryptocurrency market, where different players participate, from small traders to large investors. It helps to recognize manipulations and the intentions of large capital, allowing more effective interaction with the market.
