Best time for trading

Hello! I am Igor Arapov and today we will discuss an important topic: when is the best time to trade on the market. Many beginner traders think that you can trade at any time since the market is always open. But in reality, timing is very important.

Active Sessions

First of all, I would like to note that the European and American sessions are the most active trading hours. It is during this time, when the European and American sessions overlap, that banks process the maximum number of client orders, leading to huge trading volumes. This is a very busy time, a time of large volumes.

If you study volume market analysis, you have probably noticed that it is during these hours that some of the largest volumes enter the market. This is not surprising, as it is during this time that there is the highest activity of traders.

Key News

It is also worth mentioning that key news often comes out before the start of the American session. For example, news can be released at 15:30 when America is just waking up. Therefore, it is important for beginner traders to consider the trading hours.

Less Active Hours

Asia and the Pacific region are less active hours. Trading volumes at this time are minimal, and there is no point in sitting in front of the monitor around the clock. This is the first plus.

False Breakouts

The second plus is that false breakouts on the market often occur at the beginning of the European session. Before the session starts, market makers can make false moves to confuse traders. This is a good time to trade in the opposite direction.

Start of the American Session

The start of the American session is also an important moment. If the European session has been calm, the American session is often active and trending. Sometimes Europe stands still, which may indicate a trend is forming for the American session.

Conclusion

Thus, dividing time into time zones in trading is very useful, and many experienced traders do this.
