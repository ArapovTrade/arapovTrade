Trading and Investments. What is better?

Hello everyone! Today we will talk about trading and investing. These two concepts are often confused, and I want to help you understand the difference and not get confused when choosing your path. My name is Igor Arapov, I have been trading for over 11 years, and I will share my experience.

The Difference Between Trading and Investing

Let's start with the S&P 500 index, which has shown almost parabolic growth since 2008. The question is: by buying this index, are you investing or speculating? A trader makes money by buying cheaper and selling higher. An investor, however, earns not only from the growth but also from dividends when buying stocks.

Although trading and investing may look the same on a chart, they are different things. For example, if you compare the S&P 500 chart with the Bitcoin chart, both show explosive growth. But can you earn on Bitcoin as an investor? Hardly, because Bitcoin does not produce anything.

Classic Investing

Classic investing involves buying assets that create added value, such as factories and plants. These assets work, produce goods, and their owners receive profits in the form of dividends. An investor focuses more on stable income rather than selling the asset.

There is no such model in trading. A trader buys an asset without added value and makes money only on price differences. If the market does not rise or fall, the trader loses money. An investor, on the other hand, earns income from the operation of the asset, regardless of its market price.

Investor vs. Speculator

An investor does not wait for speculative profit. If you buy cryptocurrencies hoping for rapid growth, you are not an investor but a speculator. Investing is aimed at preserving and increasing capital, protecting it from inflation.

In the West, people actively invest to protect their money from inflation and earn income. A bank deposit does not cope with this task and carries bankruptcy risks. The stock market can bring stable income if you trust monetary policy.

During the pandemic, the Fed supported the market by printing money and buying bonds. But you cannot print money endlessly because it leads to inflation. I hope this article will be useful for beginners. It will help you understand whether you want to become a long-term investor or a trader.
