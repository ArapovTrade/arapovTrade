Types of orders on the exchange

Hello everyone! Today we will talk about an important topic – types and kinds of orders. I have already touched on this topic in previous articles about pricing and liquidity, and I recommend everyone to read them. So now let's take a closer look at what types of orders there are, how they affect the price and the formation of the order book.

Understanding these elements, known as market microstructure, is necessary for the introduction to volume market analysis. Without understanding what we are going to talk about today, it is impossible to analyze volumes, search for smart money, or large players. So, let's start.

As I have already said, the price in the market is always formed by two sides of participants. On one side are the sellers (ask or offer price), on the other side — the buyers (bid price). In the order book, the bid price shows the level at which buyers are willing to buy an asset, for example, Bitcoin. Values in the bid column usually change very quickly, especially during active trading and news when the hype is high.

It is important to understand that we consider the order book as a moment in time, as if we froze the market and analyzed what was at that moment. In the order book by price, there is a certain number of orders waiting to be executed (buyer's price), and in the ask/offer column — seller's orders. Limit orders are matched with market orders. For a seller to sell a product at the market price, there must be a buyer who, using a market order, will buy the asset at that price and volume.

Types of Orders

To match these types of orders, we need two types of orders. It may seem complicated, but I will try to explain. On one side, we have pending orders that are in the order book, and on the other — market orders that are executed here and now. A market order must be executed at the market price instantly. Pending orders are divided into limit, stop-limit, and stop orders, but market orders have only two values: buy and sell, and are executed as quickly as possible. A pending order may or may not be executed.

Impact on Price

By how they affect the price, limit buy orders are support from below, and limit sell orders are resistance from above. This is essentially a reflection of the levels on the chart, only the spread between them can change.

Market buyers with buy stops try to break the resistance and raise the price, while market sellers with sell stops try to break the support and lower the price. This is the nature of the influence of market orders on the current price. Accordingly, if a trader analyzes supply and demand, volume formation, and so on, he can determine from the order book how many buyers and sellers there are now and in which direction the imbalance will be directed. This gives a huge advantage over those who just look at the price or use indicators.

Pending Orders

About pending orders, I will add the following. They are used not only to fill the order book but also as a method of fixing profits. Large players who buy a product cheaply and want to sell it more expensively use limit orders. Large players buy on the fall with limit orders so as not to affect the price, as market orders always affect the price. If the orders are very large, the large player will get a bad execution price. Therefore, they try to buy and sell with limit orders. They buy from small participants who sell en masse at the breakout of the level, allowing large players to buy or fix profits.

Conversely, in a rising market, a large player can sell to small participants who are actively buying. This technology has an accumulation phase and a distribution phase, used in volume analysis to determine potential reversal points.

I recommend understanding how the price is formed in the market, what an offer and bid are, how market orders are matched with these orders, and what information this gives for determining the future price and prospects of large players.
