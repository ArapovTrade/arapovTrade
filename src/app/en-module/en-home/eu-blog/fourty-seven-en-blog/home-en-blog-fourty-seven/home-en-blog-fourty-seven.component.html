Fundamental Market Analysis

Hello everyone! Today we will discuss the basics of market analysis using the Wyckoff method. Many of you ask: "Why do we focus only on technical analysis, not considering news, corporate reports, and other factors?" The answer is simple: as retail traders, we operate with information available through charts, without the privilege of accessing primary data available to big market players. Therefore, fundamental analysis is not as important for us. This video is for those interested in news and statistics.

Why don't we engage in fundamental analysis? Let's start with a small excerpt from Wyckoff's book that will help us understand this question:

"Large operators differ from small participants in their ability to foresee significant changes in stock prices six months or a year in advance and prepare for them. Studies of these preparatory periods show that understanding market anatomy, the direction of its future movement, and possible magnitude are key to successful trading. This study of turning points, growth, and panic is extremely important. Small participants must realize that the actions of those trading large volumes of securities determine their profits. Understanding market conditions helps develop such foresight. Knowledge of fundamental aspects, such as politics, company earnings, and others, often does not provide accurate forecasts. But this does not mean that beginners should ignore them. Understanding the positions of large operators is more important than fundamental data."

This excerpt explains that in the stock market, large players can anticipate future price movements and prepare for them in advance, using their resources and knowledge. They react to future asset values, while small traders often react to current events.

Therefore, our efforts are focused on technical analysis, which allows us to track changes in prices and trading volumes. We look for signals and patterns that help us make decisions about entering or exiting positions. This helps us minimize risks and improve our results.

Thus, fundamental factors are important, but for us, the key is understanding the technical aspects of the market, which help us make informed decisions.
