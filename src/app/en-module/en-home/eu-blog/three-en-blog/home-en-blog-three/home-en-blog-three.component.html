Volatility in trading

Hello, friends! I'm Igor Arapov, and today we will talk about market volatility. What is volatility, how to use it, and why we need the ATR (Average True Range) indicator.

First, it's important to understand that there is always volatility in the market. It can be low, medium, or high. In one day, the market can move 10, 40, or even 100 points. This indicator constantly changes, and this is exactly what traders earn from. Without volatility, when the market is stagnant, trading is impossible.

The market has phases: expansion and contraction. For example, at the beginning of the European session, when the price contracts like a spring, a false breakout often occurs, which can be a signal for action.

Market volatility constantly alternates: first comes contraction, then expansion, and so on. This is the immutable mechanics of the market.

So, if we understand this, we can use the ATR indicator over 14 bars. This indicator measures the average range of market movement, showing how volatile or calm the market is. At these extreme values, you can fix your trades.

ATR does not predict a reversal, but it shows when the market has reached its peak volatility. When the indicator shows a high value, it means the market has been moving in one direction for a long time and may soon stop or correct.

Any trend trader should know this indicator to exit the market in time before a correction. Otherwise, all profits can be lost.

ATR calculates its values simply. It takes the last 14 bars, sums their highs and lows, and divides by 14 to get the average value. If the indicator shows high values, be ready to exit the market.

That's all you need to know about volatility and the ATR indicator.
