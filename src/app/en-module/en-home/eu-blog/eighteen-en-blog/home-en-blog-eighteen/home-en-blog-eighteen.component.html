Where does the volume itself come from? Why do we see it on the charts? And can we trust the information it provides us?

Volumes on Charts

Now to the volumes themselves. You see them at the bottom of the screen. Now I will show you the data of a gold futures from the CME exchange. These volumes are confirmed and reflect the real number of contracts sold over two hours. Each bar on the chart represents a two-hour interval, and you see how many contracts were executed during this period.

Volume Analysis

Let's analyze what we see here. If large players are actively trading large volumes and trying to move the market upwards, we will see that the buying volumes prevail. This is logical. Volumes help us understand when demand exceeds supply and vice versa.

If professionals are not interested in growth and prefer to sell, we will see a decline in volumes on price increases. This indicates that there is no support for the continued growth, and the price is likely to decrease.

Conclusion

I hope this article helped you understand why so many traders use volumes for market analysis. This is not just statistics, but a tool that allows you to understand the intentions of large players and make informed decisions in the market.
