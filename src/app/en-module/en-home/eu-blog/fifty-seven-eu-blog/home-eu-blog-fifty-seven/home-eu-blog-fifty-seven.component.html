Indicators in trading

Hello, friends! My name is Igor Arapov, and today I want to talk about indicators in trading. I have been trading for over 11 years, and I want to share my experience and advice on using indicators in trading.

Most of us enter trading through indicators. It's a popular topic in trading books. There are many different indicators, and beginner traders often take them at face value. But with time and experience, many begin to doubt their effectiveness. I went through this too, and during my first three years, I actively used various indicators until I abandoned them, except for the volume indicator. Those who watch my videos know that apart from volume, I don't use anything else for forecasting.

Why did I abandon indicators? Let's break it down.

There are two types of indicators: oscillators, which catch market reversals, and trend indicators, which predict the trend direction. The market has two key states: flat (reversals up and down) and trend (directional movement). The problem is that indicators lag behind. To get a signal from an indicator, you need to wait for the bar to form, and you don't know what it will be. So, you're always chasing the market.

For example, a trend indicator like the moving average sometimes captures good trends. When the price falls, and the indicator shows downward movement, you can stay in a profitable position for a long time. But an oscillator like the stochastic might show overbought or oversold conditions, leading to opposite signals. The question arises: which one to trust â€” the moving average or the stochastic? If one indicator shows profit and the other loss, it raises doubts about their use. Sometimes they work, sometimes they don't.

There are many indicators that try to analyze the market comprehensively, but they also lag. An example might be the volatility indicator ATR (Average True Range), which shows how volatile the market is compared to previous days. It's useful for locking in profits but not for predicting price movement.

Volumes, on the other hand, allow you to understand the intentions of market participants. By looking at volumes, we can see how much was bought or sold and identify the presence of large players. This helps predict future price movements based on current supply and demand. In this regard, volumes outperform any indicator because they allow you to look into the future rather than analyze the past.

After many years of experimentation, I concluded that it's best to use volumes when analyzing the market. I advise beginner traders to continue experimenting with indicators to gain experience. But remember, indicators focused on tracking price will always lag. It's better to use tools that help predict future price movements, such as volumes.
