Why is trading so difficult?

Hello everyone! My name is Igor Arapov and today I want to talk about trading psychology. Those of you who follow my channel know that I share my experience through educational videos available for free for self-study.

In this video, I want to share my thoughts on the journey of becoming a trader and its development. Today we have a somewhat philosophical approach. I just want to think aloud, recall my experience, and draw key conclusions.

Once, in 2013 (now 2024), I started trading. Over these 11 years, much has changed, but the basics remain unchanged: “Japanese candlesticks”, “bars”, “volumes”, “levels” — all the same.

Professional money that existed then is the same now. The infrastructure has, of course, evolved: more accurate robots and new technologies have appeared. But for the ordinary trader, the basic principles remain unchanged. I have highlighted key points on my chart, the methodology is available on my Telegram channel for free download and study.

The market continues to fluctuate, beginners come and go, many lose deposits, but some become successful traders after training and experience.

Reflecting on this, I realized that paid training is preferable to free training due to the important role of a mentor. Beginner traders often take risks due to a lack of experience and knowledge, but a mentor helps them develop the right skills and manage their emotions.

Psychology plays a key role in trading. From the absence of fear in beginners to its gradual accumulation with experience — this whole process requires support and mentorship.

So, if you are thinking about trading, learn from those who have already gone through this path. It can become a decisive moment in your success in the market.
