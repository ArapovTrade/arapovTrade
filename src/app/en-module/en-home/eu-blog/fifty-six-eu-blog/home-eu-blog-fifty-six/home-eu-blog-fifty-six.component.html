What are the benefits of books on trading for beginners?

Hello, friends! My name is Igor Arapov, and today I want to talk about trading books.

I know that many people learn trading on their own, but of course, we need to mention trading books, as they are also important for self-education. Videos can be more specific, of course, but books are also useful, especially from the perspective of psychological support.

For example, when you read good trading books like "Long-Term Secrets" by Larry Williams, you feel that you are not alone. Larry Williams is a legend in trading, and his book shows how he overcame difficulties, lost money, but didn't give up and achieved success. This is inspiring.

I have always emphasized that the main advantage of paid education is the support of the teacher. Beginners often struggle, make mistakes, and this can be very hard on their self-esteem. At such moments, a good teacher can provide support. Good books can also serve this role. They remind you that mistakes are part of the journey and that there is light at the end of the tunnel.

I personally started with the book "Trading Chaos" by Bill Williams. It impressed me a lot at the time, but in practice, it turned out that things were not as simple as described. This was a mixed experience for me: at first, I believed the book and was disappointed, but then I found a book that honestly talked about the difficulties. Reading such books helped me understand that mistakes are normal, and the main thing is not to give up.

Good books help you deal with crises. When you're trading and taking losses, you can open a book and find support there. This is very valuable for a beginner trader. However, it's important to remember that books are often written in general terms and rarely cover specific cases. The market has many different situations, and books can't cover them all.

I recommend studying volume analysis to develop confidently and logically think about the intentions of market participants. This will help you make money.
