This is the full catalog of educational videos on the @ArapovTrade channel: 78+ lessons, sorted into nine categories, with descriptions and direct links. I have run the channel and traded by one method since 2013, so the lessons here are not a random pile but a sequence: each next one rests on the previous. The videos went up between June 2023 and December 2025, and below they are laid out so you know what to watch and in what order.

Three routes through the channel

Before diving into individual videos, pick a route for your level. Each runs from simple to complex.

Beginner route. First what trading is and how to start, then the basic concepts (levels, technical analysis, order types), and the block on how not to blow the account is a must. Plug psychology in from the start, not at the end.

Intermediate route. Wyckoff and volume, then Smart Money and liquidity grabs, then building your own system with clear rules for entry, exit and risk.

Advanced route. If you want it all at once, watch the flagship course of two hours twenty-five minutes: from market basics to a full system on volume analysis and Smart Money.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are all the videos really free?

Yes, the whole channel is free, no hidden payments or paywall. The only paid thing I offer is mentorship as a personal format, but for a self-guided start the videos and articles are enough.

 Which video should a beginner start with?

With Category 6 and the block on how not to lose money. First understand how the market works and master the stop-loss, only then move to Wyckoff and Smart Money. If you want it all at once, watch the flagship two-hour-twenty-five course.

 In what order should I watch the lessons?

By the route for your level, top to bottom: basics and risk, then volume and Wyckoff, then Smart Money and building a system. Plug psychology in from the start, what drains accounts is more often emotion than theory.

 What language is the channel in?

The videos are in Russian, but the chart examples are universal and apply to any market. A text version of the same topics is on arapov.trade in three languages, including English.

 Do I need indicators if the method is volume-based?

As signals, in my view, no. I trade by volume and levels and dropped every indicator except volume ones. They lag, they are derivatives of price. The exception is VWAP, a volume benchmark.
