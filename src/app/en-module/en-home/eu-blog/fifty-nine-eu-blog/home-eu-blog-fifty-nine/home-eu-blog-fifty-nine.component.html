How to trade Level Breakout in trading

Hello everyone! My name is Igor Arapov, and today I’ll be talking about breakouts. I’ve previously written about working with levels and the pin bar. Today, I want to combine these topics and discuss the pin bar, volume, and breakouts. This comprehensive approach will help you understand how and where to open a trade.

If you prefer the breakout technique, the first step is to identify the levels you want to trade. Levels are key points that all market participants pay attention to. If you’re looking to trade a breakout and sell before the next local level, you need to wait for a few steps.

First, wait for the breakout of the level, and then the retest. So, it’s breakout, retest, and only then do you enter. Otherwise, the price might just bounce back up, leaving you in a losing trade as the local trend reverses sharply.

What should you look for after the pullback? The pin bar is an excellent pattern in technical analysis using Japanese candlesticks. It always indicates the market's inability to move in either direction.

If we expect buyers to control the market, this pattern should not appear at the level. It’s a sign of weakness, showing that buyers are unable to push prices higher. This is exactly the setup you should be looking for when trading a breakout.

First, wait for the breakout, preferably on high volume. High volume indicates that market participants are liquidating their positions at a loss, their stops are being hit, and the price is dropping. After this, many traders try to buy on the pullback.

When you see the pullback, pay attention to the top of the retracement. If a pin bar or upthrust appears there, it’s a signal that buyers are exhausted, and the price is unlikely to go higher. This is your entry point. Place your sell order below the pin bar and your stop above it.

Your risk-to-reward ratio should be no less than 1 to 2. Ideally, choose quality levels where you can achieve a ratio of 1 to 3.

The pin bar is a key pattern after a breakout. Once it forms, expect the price to move in its direction and trade in line with the breakout.
