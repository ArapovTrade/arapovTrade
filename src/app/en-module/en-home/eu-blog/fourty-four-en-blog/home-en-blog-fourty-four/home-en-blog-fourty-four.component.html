Trading Basics for Beginners

Hello! Are you just starting your journey in trading? Then this article is for you. My name is Igor Arapov, and I have been trading in the market for 11 years.

The first thing you need to understand is where you have come. You have come to a market where prices for goods constantly change. Your goal is to make money from these changes. Trading is pure speculation. The trader's task is to buy low and sell high, or vice versa: sell high and then buy low. You can make money in the market both on rising and falling prices. But to do this, you need to learn to forecast the market. Without this, unfortunately, nothing will work.

Mistakes are inevitable, especially at the beginning, and you will realize that you can't just master the market. This is a profession, and any profession requires knowledge. You need to learn trading to be able to forecast the market: where to buy, where to sell, and why exactly this way. This is all connected to the trading system you need to become familiar with.

Many beginners do the following: they open a trade, the price moves up and down, they wait for the price to return to the plus, exit, and rejoice, thinking they have made money. But this is a mistaken understanding of the profession. The price of assets often changes sharply and significantly, and does not always return. For example, if you bought Bitcoin at 60,000, thinking it would rise, and hope to wait it out if the price falls, I want to warn you. Central banks control monetary policy, i.e., the amount of money in the economy. When there is a lot of money, assets rise, and Bitcoin rises too. But when the policy changes and there is less money, market participants withdraw their funds, and markets fall. Bitcoin can be worth 60,000, 100,000, or 10,000 for an indefinite time. It all depends on the cycle of monetary policy. This may sound complicated, but you must understand that you are entering a profession where banks make their profits and are professionals in this market. We determine the direction of volumes, read the balance of supply and demand to make money too. One way or another, you will have to learn. The craft is not acquired without theory and training. Prepare yourself to spend time learning, but it will pay off later.

If you just trade without a systematic approach, it will most likely end in failure. Any experienced trader who has been in the market for more than six months will confirm this. In my 11 years, I have made many mistakes, and I want to say that if you want to protect yourself from my mistakes, start correctly. I started by reading on the internet and watching videos, but it ended badly. Approach this profession systematically, as if you were studying at a university. By gaining fundamental knowledge, you will earn steadily. If you choose another path, it will most likely end badly, and you will have a negative experience.

The longer you are in the profession, the better the cumulative effect works, you gain experience, and with each new trade, you earn more. Look at this business as a long-term project. Do not try to earn quickly, develop your small business — your deposit, which you strive to increase. Growth will be slow at the beginning, but in the long run, the logic will work, and you will be satisfied.

All rules are written by traders' losses, and they must not be broken if you want to be successful. Learn, and you will succeed.
