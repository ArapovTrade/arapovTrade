Pin-bar or up-trust

In volume analysis — is a bar with a tail. It is very important to understand that a Pin-bar is a trap bar, meaning the market uses the Pin-bar to catch participants, forcing them to enter on the wrong side of the market. Hence the name "Pinocchio-bar" or "I fooled you". To not be fooled by the market, you need to understand the current market trend. The main application of the Pin-bar is to hint at where the local trend will develop. The Pin-bar is formed on the chart under the influence of demand and supply. By analyzing the efforts of demand or supply, we can understand who is weaker and who is stronger in the market. Every time you see shadows on top and the price drops, you can be sure that the buyer is weak. The weakness of the buyer is determined by the fact that he tried to raise the price, but he couldn't, and the supply in this range flooded the demand. Therefore, the Pin-bar is an important signal indicating the direction the trend is developing. You can never argue with the trend, as the trend may develop down to the local support level or go much lower. If you work against the trend, you will have big problems. Always rely on Pin-bars as signals in the trend. They are always the market peaks, that is, in any trend, if we are talking about a bearish trend, they will be the peaks. You can set a stop-loss behind these shadows, which limits your risk if you have entered into a sale.

Unfortunately, in the news Pin-bars often deceive the market because the news has high volatility, the order book empties and these figures work poorly there. Therefore, during the news, it is better not to use them. But when the market is liquid, that is, when the order book is full and a normal trading session is going on, these figures are an excellent signal indicating the direction the trend is developing.

In this example, we are discussing gold. You can see by the volumes that the buyer tried to buy back the sales. Every time the buyer tries to do something, he gives waves up, trying to raise gold, but each wave ends with a Pin-bar. This suggests that the level is likely to be broken down and the price will go lower.

In volume analysis, we can open a short deal, set a stop behind the Pin-bar and wait for the decrease to the nearest support. I hope that in this example I explained how to use the Pin-bar.
