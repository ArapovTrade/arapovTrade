For many beginners, setting a stop-loss becomes a problem due to uncertainty about where to properly place it, which often leads to ignoring this tool. As a result, the market can move against them so strongly that losses grow and the account begins to decrease rapidly. Today we will understand how to avoid such situations, what rules should be followed to minimize drawdowns, and how to properly use averaging positions to get out of them.

Let's start with the basics. A drawdown occurs when a trader does not consider the current market trend. For example, if we look at the NASDAQ index, it is obviously growing. Simple technical analysis shows an increase in lows, which indicates a strong growth perspective. If you open selling positions or enter the market at lows, it may later turn out that the market has moved up significantly and it will be difficult to correct the mistake.

Therefore, the following rule is important: never trade against the current trend. Trending markets usually move in one direction for a long time. If you get stuck in such a market, you will have to wait a long time, which can negatively affect your psychological state and your balance. You will have to constantly add money to the account to survive in such a situation. Even when the market begins to change, traders who ignore the current trend often do not notice it in time.

The problem is that traders often work against the market. For example, when prices are rising, they sell, and when prices are falling, they buy. It is very important to understand that these transactions often turn out to be unprofitable because there is no support from levels that can be used for averaging. The market often moves far, not giving traders in the current trend a chance to fix the profit.

Therefore, attempts to catch a trend reversal are often ineffective and costly. My recommendation: always check whether you are trading against the current trend. If the market signals the start of a new trend (for example, through technical analysis), it is better to close the current positions. After the market confirms the change of trend, you can think about the possibility of averaging positions, but always in the direction of the current trend. Statistics show that this helps to avoid significant losses.
