Smart Money - the strategy used by banks to trade

Hello everyone! My name is Igor Arapov and today we are continuing to discuss the “Smart Money” strategy. In this lesson, we will learn how to identify entry points on charts. As I have mentioned before, volume analysis helps us track the activity of “Smart Money” in the market. Our task is to analyze trading volume in conjunction with price to determine which side of the market the big players are on.

Let's start with the basics. I have often emphasized the importance of identifying manipulations and entry points at levels, because levels reflect the value of an asset. Smart money aims to buy at the most favorable prices, and the more, the better. However, fortunately for us as retail traders, they are limited by available liquidity. Liquidity is a key aspect of the market, showing how much can be bought or sold without significantly impacting the price. We can make large trades without greatly affecting the price, whereas large investors cannot boast of this.

When “Smart Money” starts accumulation, it usually happens at the end of a downtrend, when market sentiment is very pessimistic, and traders are inclined to sell, fearing further losses. This is the beginning of the accumulation phase. Using volume analysis, we can with high probability determine where this phase begins and ends.

Liquidity tends to run out. When there is a shortage of supply and demand, it is only a matter of time. Where “Smart Money” starts accumulation, a balance eventually turns into an imbalance. This term means a change in the market state from equilibrium to imbalance. These moments indicate an upcoming trend change. To determine future trend changes, we often only need to see two key volume signals: the presence or absence of liquidity in a certain price range. This forces big capital to influence the price.

Before liquidity disappears, “Smart Money” often provokes a false breakout of the level to force everyone who bought in this range to exit their trades.

The idea of the “Smart Money” strategy is to look for levels where there is a liquidity shortage — whether in an uptrend or downtrend. Once we identify such a shortage, we can be sure that Smart Money will soon start taking active actions to change prices. We already know that where there is not enough supply, buyers are forced to influence the price. Therefore, any news or data that can affect the market represents an opportunity for us. As soon as you see a similar pattern — a liquidity shortage and a false breakout — you will be ready for a trend change.

Your task is to be able to spot such moments, mark the price levels where accumulation possibly occurred, even though we cannot be 100% sure due to limitations related to open interest on the exchange in real-time. Exchanges often hide open interest in real-time to avoid issues with large volumes of trades. But we understand that where big capital is interested, there is usually a liquidity shortage. This is something that the exchange cannot hide — it is an obvious reality. And we can use this to our advantage.
